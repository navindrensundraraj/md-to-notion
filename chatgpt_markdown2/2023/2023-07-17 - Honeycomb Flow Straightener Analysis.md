This research aims to numerically investigate the effectiveness of a honeycomb device as a flow straightener in a water tunnel and evaluate its impact on flow uniformity at various flow rates. 

The study employs computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations to model the water tunnel with the integrated honeycomb flow straightener. The honeycomb device is designed to mitigate turbulent flow and streamline the water's velocity profile. A comprehensive parametric analysis is conducted, varying flow rates to examine its influence on flow uniformity.

The research outcomes provide valuable insights into the performance of the honeycomb flow straightener and its ability to maintain flow uniformity across a range of flow rates. The obtained CFD results are compared against baseline cases without a flow straightener to quantify the improvements achieved through the honeycomb device.

The findings from this study will serve as a critical reference for researchers and engineers working in the field of fluid dynamics and water tunnel design.
