# Reducing Router Max Connections

---
**User:**

What happens if i reduce max access number on my router

---
**Assistant:**

Reducing the maximum access number (also known as maximum connections or concurrent connections) on your router limits the number of devices or connections that can be active on your network simultaneously. This can have several effects:

1. Device Limitations: If you set a low maximum access number, some devices may not be able to connect to the network if the limit is reached. This can lead to connectivity issues for devices trying to join.

2. Slower Performance: A lower maximum access number can help prevent network congestion and may result in improved performance for the connected devices. However, setting it too low can also hinder performance and lead to slower speeds for users.

3. Network Security: Limiting the number of concurrent connections can be a security measure to mitigate potential threats, like denial-of-service (DoS) attacks. However, you should balance security with usability.

4. Gaming and P2P: If you're an online gamer or use peer-to-peer (P2P) applications, setting the maximum access number too low can cause problems, as these applications often require multiple connections.

5. Guest Networks: Be cautious when setting low connection limits for guest networks, as this could lead to guest users being unable to connect.

Ultimately, the ideal maximum access number depends on your network's specific needs. It's best to experiment and find a balance that works for your network without causing connectivity issues. Keep in mind that modern routers often handle a large number of connections effectively, so adjusting this setting may not be necessary for most users.
