2. Research the organization: Gather information about the organization, its values, mission, and any recent achievements or initiatives. Understand their educational approach or any specific programs they offer.

3. Prepare your responses: Anticipate common interview questions and practice your answers. Focus on showcasing your strengths, achievements, and experiences relevant to the role. 9. Plan your logistics: Double-check the interview time, format (in-person, phone, or video), and any technical requirements. If it's a virtual interview, test your internet connection, microphone, and camera in advance to avoid any last-minute issues.
