# Problem Definition Vs. Statement

---
**User:**

What is the difference between problem definition and problem statement

---
**Assistant:**

A problem definition is a clear and concise explanation of the problem being faced or the issue that needs to be addressed. It provides a general understanding of the problem, including its context, causes, and consequences. 

A problem statement, on the other hand, is a more specific and focused description of the problem that outlines its purpose, criteria for success, and stakeholders involved. It also defines the scope of the problem and identifies the root cause(s) of the issue. The problem statement serves as the basis for developing a solution and guides the project team in their efforts to resolve the issue.

In short, a problem definition provides a general understanding of the problem, while a problem statement outlines the specific problem to be solved and its requirements for a successful solution.
