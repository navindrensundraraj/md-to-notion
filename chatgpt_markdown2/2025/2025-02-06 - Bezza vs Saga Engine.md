The **Perodua Bezza** and **Proton Saga** use different types of timing systems in their engines:  

### **1. Perodua Bezza – Timing Chain**  
- Both the **1.0L and 1.3L Dual VVT-i** engines in the Bezza use a **timing chain**.  
- **Pros of Timing Chain:**  
  ✅ Longer lifespan (typically lasts the lifetime of the engine)  
  ✅ Lower maintenance (no need for periodic replacement)  
  ✅ More durable and resistant to wear  

- **Cons of Timing Chain:**  
  ❌ Can be noisier than a timing belt  
  ❌ More expensive to replace if it ever fails (but rare)  

---

### **2. Proton Saga – Timing Belt**  
- The **1.3L VVT engine** in the Saga uses a **timing belt**.  
- **Pros of Timing Belt:**  
  ✅ Quieter operation  
  ✅ Cheaper to replace compared to a timing chain  

- **Cons of Timing Belt:**  
  ❌ Needs replacement every **90,000 - 100,000 km** (or 5 years)  
  ❌ Can break if not maintained, leading to serious engine damage  

---

### **Which is Better?**  
- **Bezza’s timing chain** is more durable and requires less maintenance, making it the better choice for long-term ownership.  
- **Saga’s timing belt** requires periodic replacement but runs quieter.  

If you prefer a car with lower long-term maintenance costs, **Bezza (timing chain) is better**. But if you don’t mind replacing the timing belt every few years, **Saga is still a solid choice**.
