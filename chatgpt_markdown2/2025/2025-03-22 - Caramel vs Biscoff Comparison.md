Caramel and Biscoff are both sweet treats but have distinct flavors and uses:  

- **Caramel**: Made by heating sugar until it melts and turns golden brown, often combined with butter and cream for a rich, buttery, and slightly burnt sweetness. It is used in desserts like caramel sauce, caramel candies, and caramel lattes.  

- **Biscoff (Speculoos)**: A spiced, caramelized biscuit with notes of cinnamon, nutmeg, and brown sugar. It has a crunchy texture but can also be made into a smooth Biscoff spread. It's popular with coffee, as a cookie butter, or as a topping for desserts.  

If you like deep, buttery sweetness, go for caramel. If you prefer warm, spiced sweetness with a hint of crunch, Biscoff is the way to go!
