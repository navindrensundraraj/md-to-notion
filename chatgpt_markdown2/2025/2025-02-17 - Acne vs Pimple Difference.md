**Acne** and **pimples** are related but not the same:  

- **Acne** is a chronic skin condition caused by excess oil production, clogged pores, bacteria, and inflammation. It can lead to different types of breakouts, including pimples, blackheads, whiteheads, cysts, and nodules.  
- **Pimples** are a type of acne lesion that occur when pores become clogged with oil, dead skin cells, and bacteria. They appear as red, inflamed spots and can sometimes contain pus.  

In short, acne is the overall condition, while pimples are one of the symptoms of acne.

Treating acne depends on its severity and type. Here are some effective treatments:  

### **1. Skincare Routine**  
- **Gentle Cleansing**: Wash your face twice daily with a mild, non-comedogenic cleanser. Since you have oily, pimple-prone, and eczema-prone skin, look for a sulfate-free, fragrance-free cleanser.  
- **Oil-Free Moisturizer**: Continue using your **CeraVe lotion**, but consider one with **niacinamide** for oil control and soothing effects.  
- **Sunscreen**: Use a lightweight, non-comedogenic **SPF 30+ sunscreen** to prevent post-acne marks.  

### **2. Over-the-Counter (OTC) Treatments**  
- **Benzoyl Peroxide**: Kills acne-causing bacteria and reduces inflammation.  
- **Salicylic Acid**: Exfoliates dead skin cells and unclogs pores.  
- **Niacinamide**: Controls oil production and reduces redness.  
- **Adapalene (Differin)**: A gentle retinoid that helps prevent clogged pores.  

### **3. Prescription Treatments (For Moderate to Severe Acne)**  
If OTC treatments don’t work, consult a dermatologist for:  
- **Topical Retinoids (Tretinoin, Adapalene, or Tazarotene)**: Unclogs pores and prevents new breakouts.  
- **Antibiotics (Clindamycin, Doxycycline, or Minocycline)**: Reduces inflammation and bacteria.  
- **Hormonal Therapy (For Women)**: Birth control pills or **spironolactone** can help balance hormones.  
- **Isotretinoin (Accutane)**: A powerful treatment for severe acne but has strong side effects.  

### **4. Lifestyle Changes**  
- **Diet**: Reduce dairy and high-glycemic foods (sugary snacks, white bread) that can trigger acne.  
- **Hydration**: Drink plenty of water (without adding too much salt).  
- **Stress Management**: Stress increases oil production and breakouts, so try relaxation techniques.  
- **Hygiene**: Change pillowcases frequently and avoid touching your face.
