# Rust on Brake Disc

---
**User:**

Rust on my brake disc

---
**Assistant:**

Rust on your brake disc is quite common, especially if the car has been sitting idle for a while or exposed to moisture. Here’s what you can do:  

### **If it's light surface rust:**  
- **Drive the car**: Normal braking will wear off light rust. A short drive with a few gentle brake applications should clean the discs.  
- **Brake firmly**: Find a safe, open road and apply the brakes moderately a few times to scrape off the rust.  

### **If rust persists or is severe:**  
- **Inspect the disc**: If rust has caused pitting or deep corrosion, the disc may need resurfacing or replacement.  
- **Manual cleaning**: Use a wire brush or fine sandpaper (400-600 grit) with brake cleaner to remove stubborn rust.  
- **Check brake pads**: If the rust is excessive, it could wear down the pads unevenly.  

### **Preventing Future Rust:**  
- **Drive regularly**: Even a short drive every few days helps.  
- **Dry your brakes**: After washing the car or driving in the rain, apply the brakes lightly before parking to dry them.  
- **Rust-resistant coatings**: Some high-performance rotors come with anti-rust coatings.
