Yes, you **can** transition from a **manufacturing process engineer in encapsulation** to a **data center job**, but success depends on:

### 1. **Which Data Center Role?**
Data centers have a wide range of roles, and your suitability depends on which one you’re aiming for:

#### a. **Facilities/Operations Engineer**
- **Most aligned** with your background.
- Involves monitoring and maintaining electrical/mechanical systems (cooling, power, fire suppression).
- Skills from process engineering (e.g., monitoring, maintenance, SOPs, troubleshooting) **translate well**.
- Certifications like **BICSI, DCEP, or Uptime Institute** can help.

#### b. **Data Center Technician (Hardware)**
- Installs and maintains physical servers, network gear, cables, etc.
- Requires some IT knowledge (hardware, Linux, networking basics).
- Doable with self-study or certifications like **CompTIA A+**, **Cisco CCNA**, or **Google IT Support**.

#### c. **IT/Cloud/Systems Engineer**
- These are **more IT/software-heavy** roles (e.g., managing virtual machines, cloud infrastructure).
- A much **steeper learning curve**; you’d need IT experience, coding/scripting (Python, Bash), cloud certs (AWS, Azure).

---

### 2. **What Transferable Skills Do You Bring?**
- **Process improvement** and **SOP development** – valuable for optimizing data center operations.
- **Monitoring system performance**, logs, root cause analysis.
- **Shift work, cleanroom familiarity**, following strict protocols – directly relatable to data center environments.
- **Safety/Quality awareness** – crucial in uptime-sensitive environments.

---

### 3. **How to Make the Switch?**

1. **Target the right entry point** – Look for:
   - Data Center Operations/Facilities roles
   - Junior Data Center Technician roles
   - DC project support roles in companies like **AWS, Google, Microsoft, NTT, Equinix, TM One**.

2. **Get certifications (1-3 months)**
   - **Basic**: CompTIA A+ or Google IT Support
   - **Facility-specific**: Uptime Institute Accredited Tier Specialist, DCEP (Data Center Energy Practitioner)
   - **Optional (network path)**: CCNA, Network+

3. **Revamp your resume**
   - Highlight equipment management, monitoring, uptime reliability, root cause analysis, etc.
   - Emphasize **fast-paced, high-reliability process work** in cleanroom or semiconductor context.

4. **Network**
   - LinkedIn: Connect with data center engineers in your region.
   - Attend local tech meetups or webinars related to DC infrastructure.

Yes, **especially in infrastructure or facilities roles**. Many large data centers are open to candidates with mechanical, electrical, or manufacturing backgrounds **who show interest, reliability, and technical aptitude**.
