The early symptoms of pregnancy can vary from person to person, but common signs in the first few weeks include:  

1. **Missed Period** – One of the earliest and most reliable signs if you have regular cycles.  
2. **Implantation Bleeding** – Light spotting or pink/brown discharge about 6–12 days after ovulation.  
3. **Fatigue** – Feeling unusually tired due to rising progesterone levels.  
4. **Nausea & Morning Sickness** – Can start as early as 4–6 weeks, with or without vomiting.  
5. **Breast Changes** – Tender, swollen, or sensitive breasts with darkening of the nipples.  
6. **Frequent Urination** – Increased need to pee due to hormonal changes.  
7. **Food Cravings or Aversions** – Sudden preference or dislike for certain foods.  
8. **Mood Swings** – Emotional changes due to hormone fluctuations.  
9. **Bloating & Constipation** – Slowed digestion caused by progesterone.  
10. **Increased Basal Body Temperature** – If tracking, a consistently high BBT after ovulation may indicate pregnancy.  

A home pregnancy test can confirm pregnancy as early as the first missed period. If you suspect pregnancy, seeing a doctor for confirmation is recommended.

**BBT (Basal Body Temperature)** is your body's lowest resting temperature, usually measured in the morning before any activity.  

### **How BBT Relates to Pregnancy:**  
- After ovulation, **progesterone** causes a slight increase (about 0.3–0.6°C or 0.5–1°F) in BBT.  
- If pregnancy occurs, BBT remains elevated for more than **14 days** after ovulation.  
- If pregnancy does not occur, BBT drops before or during your period.  

### **Tracking BBT for Pregnancy Detection:**  
1. Use a **BBT thermometer** (more sensitive than a regular one).  
2. Take your temperature **at the same time every morning** before getting out of bed.  
3. Record daily readings in a chart or an app.  
4. A **consistent high BBT beyond two weeks** may indicate pregnancy.  

BBT tracking is often used for fertility awareness but is not the most reliable pregnancy test. A **home pregnancy test** is a more accurate way to confirm pregnancy.

BBT itself doesn’t directly cause coldness, but **hormonal changes during the menstrual cycle and pregnancy** can affect body temperature perception.  

### **Why You Might Feel Cold When BBT is High:**  
1. **Progesterone Effects** – After ovulation, progesterone raises BBT but may also cause blood vessel constriction, making hands and feet feel cold.  
2. **Increased Metabolism** – A higher BBT means your body is using more energy, which might make you feel colder.  
3. **Blood Flow Redistribution** – During early pregnancy, blood is directed more toward vital organs and the uterus, which can leave extremities feeling cold.  
4. **Thyroid Function** – If you frequently feel cold despite high BBT, it could be worth checking your thyroid, as hypothyroidism can cause cold sensitivity.  

If you’re tracking BBT and consistently feel colder than usual, it’s a good idea to monitor other symptoms and consult a doctor if needed.

Pregnancy officially begins when a fertilized egg implants into the uterine lining, which usually happens **6–12 days after ovulation**. However, the process from conception to detectable pregnancy can take some time.  

### **Timeline of Pregnancy Start:**  
1. **Fertilization (Day 0)** – Sperm meets egg in the fallopian tube (typically during ovulation).  
2. **Zygote Formation (Day 1–3)** – The fertilized egg divides and moves toward the uterus.  
3. **Blastocyst Stage (Day 4–5)** – The developing embryo reaches the uterus.  
4. **Implantation (Day 6–12)** – The embryo attaches to the uterine wall, marking the beginning of pregnancy.  
5. **hCG Production (Day 7–14)** – The body starts producing **human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG)**, the hormone detected by pregnancy tests.  

### **When Can Pregnancy Be Confirmed?**  
- **Early Symptoms**: Some women notice signs (fatigue, nausea, breast tenderness) **1–2 weeks after ovulation**.  
- **Home Pregnancy Test**: Can detect pregnancy **10–14 days after ovulation** (best taken after a missed period).  
- **Blood Test**: Can confirm pregnancy as early as **6–8 days after ovulation**.  

So, pregnancy technically "starts" at implantation, but noticeable symptoms and test confirmations take about **2–4 weeks after ovulation**.

Yes, abortion pills (medication abortion) are effective during early pregnancy. The two main medications used are:  

### **1. Mifepristone & Misoprostol (Most Common Method)**  
- **Mifepristone** blocks the hormone progesterone, stopping the pregnancy from growing.  
- **Misoprostol** causes the uterus to contract and expel the pregnancy tissue.  
- This method is **95–98% effective** when taken within the first **10 weeks (70 days)** of pregnancy.  

### **2. Misoprostol Alone** (Less Common)  
- Used when mifepristone is unavailable, but it’s slightly less effective.  
- Requires multiple doses and may have stronger cramping and bleeding.  

### **Effectiveness Based on Pregnancy Stage**  
- **Up to 7 weeks** – Highly effective (98%).  
- **7–9 weeks** – Still very effective (95%).  
- **9–10 weeks** – Works in most cases but may require a second dose.  

### **What to Expect After Taking the Pills**  
- **Cramping & Heavy Bleeding** – Starts within a few hours of misoprostol.  
- **Passing of Pregnancy Tissue** – Can include large clots and lasts a few hours to a few days.  
- **Side Effects** – Nausea, fever, chills, diarrhea, and fatigue are common.

- Severe pain not relieved by medication.
