If you're experiencing flu-like symptoms when you're hungry, it could be due to **low blood sugar (hypoglycemia)** or other underlying issues. Here are some possible explanations:  

1. **Low Blood Sugar (Hypoglycemia)** – When you go too long without eating, your blood sugar may drop, causing symptoms like chills, weakness, dizziness, and headaches, which can feel like the flu.  
2. **Hunger-Induced Fatigue** – Not eating for long periods can make you feel tired, achy, and sluggish, similar to flu symptoms.  
3. **Histamine Sensitivity** – Some people experience allergic-like reactions when they don't eat, leading to symptoms like congestion, headaches, and fatigue.  
4. **Dehydration** – If you don’t drink enough fluids, dehydration can make you feel unwell, with symptoms like dry mouth, dizziness, and weakness.  
5. **Cortisol Spikes** – Hunger can cause stress hormone (cortisol) fluctuations, which might lead to body aches, chills, and a general feeling of being unwell.  

### What to Do:  
- **Eat Regularly**: Try eating small, balanced meals throughout the day.  
- **Stay Hydrated**: Make sure you're drinking enough water (especially since you’ve mentioned thirst issues before).  
- **Monitor Blood Sugar**: If symptoms persist, check your blood sugar levels and consider seeing a doctor.  

If your symptoms are severe or happen frequently, you might want to get a medical check-up to rule out blood sugar imbalances, metabolic disorders, or food-related sensitivities.
