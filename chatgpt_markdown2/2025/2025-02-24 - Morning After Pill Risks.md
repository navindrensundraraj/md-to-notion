The morning-after pill (emergency contraception) is generally safe, but it can sometimes cause complications or side effects. Some possible issues include:  

### **Common Side Effects**  
- **Nausea & Vomiting** – If you vomit within 2 hours of taking the pill, you may need to take another dose.  
- **Irregular Bleeding** – Spotting or an early/late period is common.  
- **Cramps & Abdominal Pain** – Mild to moderate cramps can occur.  
- **Fatigue, Dizziness, & Headache** – These usually subside within a day or two.  

### **More Serious Complications**  
- **Severe Abdominal Pain** – Could indicate an ectopic pregnancy (a rare but serious condition). Seek medical help immediately.  
- **Allergic Reaction** – Signs like swelling, rash, or breathing difficulty require urgent medical attention.  
- **Menstrual Irregularities** – Some people experience prolonged cycle disruption.  

### **Reduced Effectiveness**  
- **Timing** – Works best within 24 hours but can still be effective up to 72-120 hours (depending on the type).  
- **BMI & Weight** – May be less effective for those with a higher body weight.  
- **Drug Interactions** – Some medications (e.g., certain antibiotics, epilepsy drugs) can reduce effectiveness.

### **Ectopic Pregnancy: Causes, Symptoms, and Risks**  

An **ectopic pregnancy** occurs when a fertilized egg implants outside the uterus, most commonly in the fallopian tubes. This is a medical emergency because the growing embryo can cause life-threatening complications if left untreated.  

---

### **Causes & Risk Factors**  
- **Previous ectopic pregnancy**  
- **Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)** from infections like chlamydia or gonorrhea  
- **Fallopian tube damage** (due to surgery or infections)  
- **Use of intrauterine devices (IUDs)**  
- **Hormonal factors or fertility treatments (IVF)**  
- **Smoking**  

---

### **Symptoms**  
Ectopic pregnancy symptoms usually appear **6-8 weeks after the last period**. Key warning signs include:  
- **Sharp or stabbing abdominal pain**, often on one side  
- **Vaginal bleeding** (lighter or heavier than a normal period)  
- **Shoulder pain** (due to internal bleeding irritating the diaphragm)  
- **Dizziness, weakness, or fainting** (signs of internal bleeding and shock)  

---

### **Diagnosis & Treatment**  
- **Diagnosis**: Blood tests (hCG levels), ultrasounds, and pelvic exams confirm ectopic pregnancy.  
- **Treatment**:  
  - **Medication (Methotrexate)** – Stops cell growth in early-stage cases.  
  - **Surgery (Laparoscopy or Laparotomy)** – If the fallopian tube is ruptured, emergency surgery is required.  

---

### **Complications**  
If untreated, an ectopic pregnancy can lead to:  
- **Fallopian tube rupture**, causing severe internal bleeding  
- **Infertility**, if tube removal is necessary  
- **Life-threatening shock** from blood loss  

If you suspect an ectopic pregnancy, seek **urgent medical attention**. Let me know if you need more details!
