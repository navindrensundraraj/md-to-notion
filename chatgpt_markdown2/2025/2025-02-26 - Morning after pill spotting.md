Yes, early pregnancy symptoms can appear as soon as a week after conception, but they vary from person to person. Common early signs include:  

1. **Missed period** – The most obvious sign if cycles are regular.  
2. **Implantation bleeding** – Light spotting around 6–12 days after ovulation (not everyone experiences this).  
3. **Breast changes** – Tender, swollen, or sore breasts with darkened nipples.  
4. **Fatigue** – Feeling unusually tired due to hormonal changes.  
5. **Nausea (morning sickness)** – Can start as early as 2–4 weeks after conception.  
6. **Increased urination** – More frequent trips to the bathroom.  
7. **Food aversions or cravings** – Sudden dislike or craving for certain foods.  
8. **Mood swings** – Hormonal changes may cause emotional shifts.  
9. **Mild cramping** – Similar to period cramps but without heavy bleeding.  
10. **Dizziness or headaches** – Due to hormonal fluctuations and changes in blood pressure.  

If pregnancy is suspected, taking a home pregnancy test about a week after a missed period can provide reliable results. A blood test from a doctor can confirm it even earlier. Let me know if you need more details!

**Assistant:**

Implantation typically occurs **6 to 12 days after ovulation**, with the most common timeframe being **8 to 10 days** after ovulation.  

### **How It Works:**  
1. **Ovulation (Day 14 in a 28-day cycle)** – The egg is released.  
2. **Fertilization (Within 24 hours of ovulation)** – Sperm meets the egg in the fallopian tube.  
3. **Travel to the Uterus (3–5 days after fertilization)** – The fertilized egg (zygote) moves toward the uterus, becoming a blastocyst.  
4. **Implantation (6–12 days after ovulation)** – The blastocyst attaches to the uterine lining, beginning pregnancy.  

### **Signs of Implantation:**  
- **Implantation bleeding** (light spotting, pink or brown)  
- **Mild cramping** (less intense than period cramps)  
- **Slight temperature drop** (in basal body temperature tracking)  
- **Increased cervical mucus**  

If implantation is successful, the body starts producing **hCG (human chorionic gonadotropin)**, the hormone detected in pregnancy tests.
