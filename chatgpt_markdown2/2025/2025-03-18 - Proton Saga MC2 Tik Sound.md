# Proton Saga MC2 Tik Sound

---
**User:**

Tik tik engine sound when going uphill

---
**Assistant:**

A "tik tik" sound when going uphill in your Proton Saga MC2 could be caused by several factors. Here are some possible reasons and solutions:  

### **Possible Causes:**  
1. **Engine Knock (Pre-ignition/Detonation)**  
   - Happens when the fuel-air mixture ignites too early.  
   - More common when going uphill due to higher engine load.  
   - **Solution:** Try using a higher octane fuel (e.g., RON97) and see if the sound disappears.  

2. **Valve or Tappet Noise**  
   - Worn-out or loose valve lifters/tappets can cause a ticking sound.  
   - **Solution:** Get a mechanic to check and adjust valve clearance.  

3. **Timing Chain Noise**  
   - If the timing chain is loose or worn, it can make a ticking noise.  
   - **Solution:** Inspect the timing chain and tensioner. Replace if needed.  

4. **Fuel Injector Noise**  
   - Some ticking is normal from fuel injectors, but excessive noise could indicate clogging or wear.  
   - **Solution:** Use a good fuel system cleaner or get the injectors checked.  

5. **Exhaust Leak**  
   - A small exhaust leak can cause a ticking sound that gets louder under load.  
   - **Solution:** Inspect the exhaust manifold and gasket for leaks.  

6. **Piston Slap or Rod Knock** (Less Likely)  
   - If the sound is deep and gets worse over time, internal engine wear might be the issue.  
   - **Solution:** A mechanic should inspect for severe engine wear.  

### **Next Steps:**  
- Try using RON97 fuel first.  
- If the sound persists, have a mechanic inspect the tappets, timing chain, and exhaust system.  
- If the sound is severe or gets worse, avoid hard acceleration until diagnosed.
