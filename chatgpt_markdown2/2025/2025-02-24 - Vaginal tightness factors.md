Penetration can cause discomfort or pain, especially if it is the first time, but it varies from person to person. Factors that influence pain include:  

- **Lack of Arousal & Lubrication**: If the body is not fully aroused, the vaginal muscles may be tense, leading to discomfort. Natural lubrication or using water-based lubricants can help.  
- **Tight Pelvic Muscles**: Anxiety or nervousness can cause involuntary muscle tightening, making penetration more difficult or painful.  
- **Hymen Stretching**: While the hymen doesn’t "break" like a membrane, it can stretch or tear, sometimes causing mild pain or spotting. However, some people have a more elastic hymen, and others may not feel anything at all.  
- **Underlying Conditions**: Conditions like vaginismus (involuntary muscle spasms), infections, or hormonal imbalances can contribute to pain.  

Gentle, patient, and consensual experiences, along with communication and proper foreplay, help reduce discomfort. If pain persists, consulting a healthcare professional is recommended.
