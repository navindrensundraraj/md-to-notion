To make your Proton Saga MC2 last as long as a Toyota, follow these maintenance and driving habits:

### **Regular Maintenance**
1. **Frequent Oil Changes** – Use high-quality engine oil and change it every 5,000–7,000 km (or follow Proton’s recommendation).  
2. **Transmission Care** – Change automatic transmission fluid (ATF) or manual gearbox oil as per schedule.  
3. **Cooling System** – Use good-quality coolant and flush the radiator every 40,000 km to prevent overheating.  
4. **Brake System** – Regularly check brake pads, discs, and fluid to ensure optimal braking performance.  
5. **Suspension & Alignment** – Check shock absorbers, bushings, and alignment to prevent excessive wear.  
6. **Timing Belt & Drive Belts** – Replace at the recommended intervals to avoid breakdowns.  
7. **Battery & Electricals** – Ensure proper battery maintenance and clean terminals to prevent electrical issues.  

### **Driving Habits**
1. **Gentle Acceleration & Braking** – Avoid sudden acceleration and hard braking to reduce engine and transmission stress.  
2. **Warm Up Before Driving** – Let the engine idle for 30 seconds after starting, especially in the morning.  
3. **Avoid Overloading** – Carrying excessive weight strains the engine, transmission, and suspension.  
4. **Drive Smoothly on Bumpy Roads** – Avoid potholes and rough driving to prevent chassis and suspension damage.  
5. **Don't Ignore Warning Lights** – Address any dashboard warning lights immediately to prevent major issues.  

### **Long-Term Care**
1. **Rust Prevention** – Apply undercarriage rust protection, especially if driving in humid areas.  
2. **Use Quality Parts** – Stick to OEM or high-quality aftermarket parts for longevity.  
3. **Keep It Clean** – Regular washing and waxing prevent paint damage and rust.  
4. **Monitor Fuel Quality** – Use good-quality petrol and occasionally add fuel system cleaners to prevent injector clogging.  

By following these steps, your Proton Saga MC2 can last well over 10–15 years, just like a Toyota!
