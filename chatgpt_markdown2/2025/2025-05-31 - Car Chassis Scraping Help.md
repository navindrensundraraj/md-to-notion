# Car Chassis Scraping Help

---

My car just bottomed out scratching the chassis

---
**Assistant:**

Ouch — that’s always a horrible sound. Here’s what to do next:

### **1. Pull Over and Inspect (if safe):**
- Look underneath the car for any **visible damage**, like:
  - Fluids leaking (oil, coolant, transmission fluid)
  - Hanging or bent components
  - Deep gouges or dents in the **oil pan**, **exhaust system**, or **fuel lines**

### **2. Check for Warning Signs While Driving Again:**
- Steering feels off or pulls to one side
- Strange sounds (clunking, scraping, rattling)
- Fluid warning lights on the dashboard
- Engine temperature rising unusually
- Exhaust sounds louder than usual (possible muffler or pipe damage)

### **3. Common Parts That May Be Damaged:**
- **Oil pan or transmission pan**
- **Exhaust pipe or catalytic converter**
- **Fuel lines or brake lines**
- **Chassis undertray or splash guard**
- **Suspension components** (if it was a hard hit)

### **4. What to Do Next:**
- If the car drives fine and there’s **no leaking fluid**, you may have gotten lucky with just superficial scratches.
- If you're unsure, **get it on a lift** at a workshop and ask them to inspect the underside for hidden damage — especially the oil pan and suspension.
- If oil or any fluid is leaking, **do not drive further** — tow it to avoid catastrophic engine damage.
