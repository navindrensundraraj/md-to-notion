To maximize fuel efficiency in a car with a **torque converter automatic transmission**, follow these driving techniques:  

### **1. Smooth Acceleration and Braking**  
- **Gradual Acceleration:** Avoid rapid throttle inputs; accelerate gently to prevent excessive fuel consumption.  
- **Coast to a Stop:** Instead of braking hard, let off the accelerator early and allow the car to slow down naturally before braking.  

### **2. Maintain a Steady Speed**  
- **Use Cruise Control:** On highways, cruise control helps maintain a constant speed, reducing unnecessary throttle adjustments.  
- **Avoid Sudden Speed Changes:** Frequent acceleration and deceleration consume more fuel.  

### **3. Optimal Speed & RPM Control**  
- **Stay in the Economy Zone:** Most cars are fuel-efficient between **50-80 km/h (30-50 mph)**.  
- **Lower RPMs Are Better:** Torque converter automatics slip more at lower speeds, so avoid excessive idling and slow-speed crawling.  

### **4. Minimize Torque Converter Slippage**  
- **Let the Lock-Up Clutch Engage:** Most modern automatics have a **lock-up clutch** that engages at higher speeds to reduce slippage and improve efficiency. Maintain speeds above **60 km/h (37 mph)** to enable this feature.  
- **Avoid Excessive Low-Speed Driving:** The torque converter wastes energy at slow speeds due to hydraulic losses.  

### **5. Smart Use of Gears & Transmission Mode**  
- **Use ‘D’ (Drive) Mode Efficiently:** Avoid using ‘Sport’ mode unless necessary, as it holds gears longer and consumes more fuel.  
- **Shift to Neutral at Long Stops:** If idling for over **30 seconds** (e.g., at long traffic lights), shifting to neutral can reduce load on the engine.  

### **6. Reduce Unnecessary Load & Drag**  
- **Lighten Your Car:** Remove unnecessary items from the trunk to reduce weight.  
- **Use A/C Wisely:** Air conditioning increases fuel consumption; use it efficiently at moderate temperatures.  
- **Reduce Drag:** Keep windows closed at high speeds and remove roof racks when not in use.  

### **7. Tire and Maintenance Optimization**  
- **Keep Tires Properly Inflated:** Underinflated tires increase rolling resistance and fuel consumption.  
- **Regular Maintenance:** Change engine oil, air filters, and transmission fluid as recommended to keep efficiency high.  

By combining these techniques, you can significantly improve fuel economy in your torque converter automatic car.

- Check pressure **when tires are cold** (before driving) for accurate readings.  
- Regularly inspect tire wear—if the center wears out faster, reduce pressure slightly.  

So, slightly higher pressure **helps with fuel savings**, but keep it within a safe range to maintain comfort and tire longevity.

### **1. When It Saves Fuel**  
- **Stops longer than 30 seconds** (e.g., at long traffic lights, railway crossings, or waiting in parked areas).

### **2. When It’s Not Ideal**  
- **Short stops (under 30 seconds)** → Restarting may use just as much fuel as idling briefly.

- **Cold engines** → Restarting a cold engine multiple times may cause extra wear.  

### **3. Best Practice**  
- If your car has **Auto Start-Stop**, enable it—it’s optimized for fuel savings.  
- For manual shutdowns, turn off the engine **only if you anticipate a stop longer than 30 seconds**.  
- Avoid excessive restarts in heavy traffic to prevent battery and starter wear.  

Overall, shutting the engine off during long stops **does** reduce fuel consumption, especially in city driving with frequent, extended stops.

- Cruise control is **not fuel-efficient on steep hills** because it aggressively maintains speed. Instead, control acceleration manually.

❌ **Avoid Full-Throttle Starts Frequently** – Hard launches cause excessive torque converter slippage and heat buildup.
