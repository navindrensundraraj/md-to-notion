# RFID Tag Placement Guide

---
**User:**

Rfid position headlamp or windscreen

---
**Assistant:**

The best position for an RFID tag (such as for tolls or access systems) depends on your vehicle and the system's reader sensitivity, but generally:

### **Recommended Placement:**

1. **Windscreen (Top-Center, Behind Rearview Mirror)**  
   - **Most common and effective** location.
   - Keeps the tag clear of metal obstructions.
   - Protected from weather and tampering.
   - Ideal for toll systems like Touch ‘n Go RFID in Malaysia.

2. **Headlamp (Driver's Side or Center)**  
   - Often used for motorcycles or vehicles with metallic or tinted windscreens that block RFID signals.
   - More exposed to weather, damage, or theft.
   - Easier to scan at some gates but less discreet.

### **Choose Windscreen If:**
- Your windscreen is **not heavily tinted with metal-based film**.
- You want **better protection and aesthetics**.
- The system recommends or supports it (e.g., some newer RFID systems).

### **Choose Headlamp If:**
- Your windscreen has **metallic tints or coatings** that block RFID signals.
- The reader is positioned low or at vehicle headlight level.
