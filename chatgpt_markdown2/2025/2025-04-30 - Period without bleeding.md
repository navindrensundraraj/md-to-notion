# Period without bleeding

---
**User:**

Can period occur without any bleeding/spotting

---
**Assistant:**

No, a **period (menstruation)** by definition involves **bleeding**—it's the shedding of the uterine lining through the vagina. If there is **no bleeding or spotting at all**, it is **not considered a menstrual period**.

However, some things that might feel like a period without bleeding include:

- **Ovulation pain (mittelschmerz)**: Mild cramping mid-cycle.
- **Premenstrual symptoms** (PMS): Cramps, bloating, mood swings, etc., without actual bleeding.
- **Hormonal imbalance**: Can cause skipped periods with PMS-like symptoms.
- **Pregnancy**: Rarely, some may feel period-like cramps early in pregnancy.
- **Perimenopause or PCOS**: Irregular cycles, sometimes with no bleeding.

If you're experiencing period-like symptoms with no bleeding for an extended time, it’s a good idea to consult a healthcare provider to rule out hormonal or reproductive issues.
