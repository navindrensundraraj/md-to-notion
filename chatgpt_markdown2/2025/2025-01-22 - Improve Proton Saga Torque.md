Improving the low-end torque of a Proton Saga involves optimizing the engine and drivetrain to produce more power at lower RPMs. Here are some practical ways to achieve this:

### 1. **Upgrade the Air Intake System**
   - Install a high-flow air filter or a cold air intake to improve airflow to the engine.
   - Ensure the intake path is smooth to reduce resistance.

### 2. **Exhaust System Modifications**
   - Replace the stock exhaust with a performance exhaust system that has less restrictive piping.
   - Install headers designed for better low-end torque.
   - Avoid overly large exhaust pipes, as they may reduce back pressure, which is essential for low-end torque.

### 3. **ECU Remapping/Tuning**
   - Remap the ECU to optimize fuel and ignition timing for better low-RPM performance.
   - Consider professional tuning for a custom torque-focused setup.

### 4. **Adjust Throttle Body**
   - Upgrade to a slightly larger throttle body to improve air intake without sacrificing low-end torque.
   - Clean the existing throttle body to ensure smooth operation.

### 5. **Change Gear Ratios**
   - Install a lower final drive gear ratio to improve torque at the wheels.
   - This modification will enhance acceleration but may reduce top-end speed.

### 6. **Install a Lightweight Flywheel**
   - Replace the stock flywheel with a lightweight version to reduce rotational mass.
   - This allows the engine to rev faster and deliver torque more efficiently.

### 7. **Camshaft Tuning**
   - Opt for a camshaft designed for low-RPM torque rather than high-RPM power.
   - Consult a specialist for compatibility with your engine.

### 8. **Use Higher-Octane Fuel**
   - If your Proton Saga’s engine can handle it, use higher-octane fuel for better combustion and torque.

### 9. **Regular Maintenance**
   - Keep the engine in top condition by performing regular oil changes, replacing spark plugs, and maintaining proper ignition timing.

### 10. **Turbocharging (Optional)**
   - A small turbocharger or supercharger optimized for low-end boost can significantly increase torque.
   - Ensure the engine can handle forced induction before proceeding.

### 11. **Upgrade Tires**
   - Use tires with better grip to ensure efficient torque transfer to the road.

### Notes
   - Modifications may void your warranty or affect reliability, so proceed cautiously.
   - Consult a professional mechanic or tuner for compatibility and installation.
