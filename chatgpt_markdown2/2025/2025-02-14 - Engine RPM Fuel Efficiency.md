# Engine RPM Fuel Efficiency

---
**User:**

Does keeping my engine rev below 2k save fuel?

---
**Assistant:**

Yes, keeping your engine revs below 2,000 RPM *can* help save fuel, but it depends on several factors:  

### How It Saves Fuel:  
1. **Lower RPM = Lower Fuel Consumption** – Engines generally use less fuel at lower RPMs, especially under light loads.  
2. **Avoids High Throttle Demand** – Accelerating gently and keeping RPMs low prevents excessive fuel injection.  
3. **Reduces Engine Friction** – Lower RPMs mean fewer cycles per minute, reducing overall friction and wear.  

### When It Doesn't Help:  
1. **Too Low RPM = Engine Strain** – If the engine is lugging (struggling at very low RPMs), it actually burns more fuel and increases wear.  
2. **Gear Selection Matters** – Staying in too low a gear at 2,000 RPM may waste fuel; upshifting at the right time is key.  
3. **Highway Speeds** – At cruising speeds, your engine may be at 2,000+ RPM in top gear, which is fine if it's the most fuel-efficient RPM range for your car.  

For your **Proton Saga MC2**, the best fuel efficiency usually comes from smooth acceleration, shifting gears efficiently, and maintaining a steady speed. If you drive a manual, aim to upshift early but not too early to avoid engine strain. If you have an automatic, it should naturally keep the engine in an efficient RPM range.
