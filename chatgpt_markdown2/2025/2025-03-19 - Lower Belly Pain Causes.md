**Assistant:**

Lower belly pain in women can have several causes, ranging from mild to serious. Here are some possible reasons:  

### **1. Gynecological Causes**  
- **Menstrual cramps (Dysmenorrhea)** – Common during periods  
- **Ovulation pain (Mittelschmerz)** – Happens mid-cycle  
- **Endometriosis** – Tissue similar to the uterine lining grows outside the uterus  
- **Ovarian cysts** – Can cause dull or sharp pain, especially if they rupture  
- **Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)** – Infection of reproductive organs  
- **Ectopic pregnancy** – A fertilized egg implants outside the uterus (medical emergency)  
- **Miscarriage** – Can cause cramping and bleeding  

### **2. Digestive Issues**  
- **Constipation** – Can cause bloating and discomfort  
- **Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)** – Chronic abdominal pain with diarrhea/constipation  
- **Appendicitis** – Sharp pain in the lower right abdomen (medical emergency)  
- **Urinary tract infection (UTI)** – Burning sensation when urinating, lower abdominal pain  

### **3. Other Possible Causes**  
- **Hernia** – Bulging of an organ through the abdominal wall  
- **Muscle strain** – From exercise or sudden movements  

### **When to See a Doctor**  
Seek medical attention if you experience:  
- Severe or sudden pain  
- Pain with fever, nausea, or vomiting  
- Pain during pregnancy  
- Pain with unusual vaginal bleeding or discharge
