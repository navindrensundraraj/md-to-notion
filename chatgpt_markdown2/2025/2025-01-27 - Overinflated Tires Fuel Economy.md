# Overinflated Tires Fuel Economy

---
**User:**

Over inflating tyres help with fuel economy?

---
**Assistant:**

Overinflating tires slightly can reduce rolling resistance, which can improve fuel economy to a small extent. However, this comes with significant drawbacks:

### Pros:
- **Lower Rolling Resistance**: Higher pressure reduces the contact area between the tire and the road, decreasing friction and improving mileage.

### Cons:
1. **Reduced Traction**: Less contact with the road can compromise grip, especially in wet or slippery conditions.
2. **Uneven Tire Wear**: Overinflated tires wear out more quickly in the center, shortening their lifespan.
3. **Ride Comfort**: Higher pressure makes the ride stiffer and less comfortable.
4. **Safety Risks**: Overinflated tires are more prone to blowouts, especially on rough or hot roads.

### Recommendation:
Stick to the manufacturer's recommended tire pressure for a balance of fuel economy, safety, and tire life. If you’re looking to improve fuel economy, consider other factors like proper vehicle maintenance, reducing unnecessary weight, and smoother driving habits.
