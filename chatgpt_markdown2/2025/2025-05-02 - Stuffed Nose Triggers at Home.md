# Stuffed Nose Triggers at Home

---
**User:**

I always get stuffed nose when I'm back home

---
**Assistant:**

If you consistently get a stuffed nose when you're back home, it's likely due to allergens or irritants in your home environment. Common culprits include:

1. **Dust mites** – Often found in bedding, carpets, and curtains.
2. **Mold** – Can grow in damp areas like bathrooms, kitchens, or behind walls.
3. **Pet dander** – Even if pets aren't allowed in certain areas, dander spreads easily.
4. **Poor ventilation or air quality** – Trapped indoor air can build up allergens and pollutants.
5. **Strong scents** – Like incense, scented candles, or air fresheners.

A few things to try:
- Use a **HEPA air purifier** in your bedroom or living area.
- Wash bedding weekly in **hot water**.
- Clean/replace AC filters regularly.
- Consider getting a **humidity meter**; keep humidity between 40–60%.
- If symptoms persist, an **allergy test** may help pinpoint the trigger.

**Assistant:**

Yes, you can run your AC in **dry mode**, and it might actually help your stuffy nose.

**Dry mode** reduces humidity without significantly lowering the temperature. This is useful if:

- Your home feels damp or muggy.
- You live in a humid climate.
- There's mold or dust mite growth (they thrive in humidity above 60%).

**Benefits for nasal congestion:**
- Reduces moisture that supports mold, mildew, and dust mites.
- Makes breathing easier if high humidity is a trigger.

**However**, if the air becomes **too dry** (below 40% humidity), it can also dry out your nasal passages, worsening congestion. If that happens, consider:
- Running dry mode only for a few hours.
- Using a **humidifier** or placing a bowl of water in the room if air feels too dry.

What's the intended purpose for dry mode on ac

---
**Assistant:**

The **intended purpose of dry mode** on an air conditioner is to **reduce humidity** in the room — not to cool it significantly, but to make it feel more comfortable.

Here’s how it works:
- The AC runs the **compressor and fan at a low speed**, extracting moisture from the air.
- The temperature stays roughly the same or drops slightly.
- It uses **less electricity** than normal cooling mode.

**Dry mode is ideal when:**
- The air feels muggy or sticky.
- The temperature is bearable but **humidity is high**.
- You're trying to prevent mold, mildew, or allergens like dust mites.

In Malaysia’s tropical climate, dry mode is especially useful during the **rainy season** or in **poorly ventilated rooms**.

**Why not full speed?**
- Full-speed fans may dry your nose and throat if humidity drops too much.
- Medium speed balances airflow without overcooling or irritating sinuses.
