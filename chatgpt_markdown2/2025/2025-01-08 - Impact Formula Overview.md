# Impact Formula Overview

---
**User:**

What is the formula for impact

---
**Assistant:**

The formula for *impact* can vary depending on the context (e.g., physics, social impact, or business). Here's how it is defined in some common contexts:

### 1. **Physics (Force of Impact)**
In physics, the impact force during a collision can be approximated using the formula:  
\[
F = \frac{{m \cdot \Delta v}}{{\Delta t}}
\]
Where:
- \( F \) = Impact force (N)
- \( m \) = Mass of the object (kg)
- \( \Delta v \) = Change in velocity (m/s)
- \( \Delta t \) = Duration of the collision (s)

If considering energy, you might use kinetic energy:
\[
KE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2
\]

---

### 2. **Social or Business Impact**
In qualitative terms, the *impact* can be measured using key metrics or frameworks like:  
\[
\text{Impact} = \text{Reach} \times \text{Effectiveness}
\]
Where:
- **Reach** is the number of people or entities affected.
- **Effectiveness** is the degree of change or benefit achieved.

---

### 3. **Marketing or Branding**
A simplified formula for impact in marketing might be:
\[
\text{Impact} = \text{Visibility} \times \text{Engagement}
\]
