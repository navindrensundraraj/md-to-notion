Clearing acne requires a combination of proper skincare, lifestyle adjustments, and sometimes medical treatments. Since you have oily, pimple-prone, and eczema-prone skin, you need a balanced approach that controls oil and breakouts without irritating your skin.  

### **1. Skincare Routine**  
#### **Morning Routine:**  
- **Gentle Cleanser** – Use a mild, non-comedogenic face wash (e.g., CeraVe Foaming Cleanser or La Roche-Posay Effaclar).  
- **Toner (Optional)** – A gentle, alcohol-free toner with niacinamide or green tea extract can help reduce oiliness.  
- **Treatment** – Apply a **niacinamide serum** (to control oil and redness) or **azelaic acid** (anti-inflammatory and helps with acne).  
- **Moisturizer** – Use a lightweight, **oil-free, non-comedogenic moisturizer** (e.g., CeraVe PM or La Roche-Posay Toleriane).  
- **Sunscreen** – Use an **oil-free, non-comedogenic SPF 30+ sunscreen** every morning (e.g., Biore UV Aqua Rich or Eucerin Oil Control).  

#### **Night Routine:**  
- **Cleanser** – Same as morning.  
- **Exfoliation (2-3 times a week)** – Use **BHA (salicylic acid) or PHA** to unclog pores and reduce oiliness. Avoid over-exfoliating to prevent irritation.  
- **Treatment** – Apply **adapalene (Differin) or benzoyl peroxide** to acne-prone areas. If your skin is sensitive, start with 2-3 times a week.  
- **Moisturizer** – Same as morning.  

### **2. Lifestyle Adjustments**  
- **Diet** – Reduce dairy, sugar, and processed foods, as they may trigger acne. Eat more omega-3s (salmon, walnuts) and antioxidants (green tea, berries).  
- **Hydration** – Drink enough water to help detoxify your skin.  
- **Stress Management** – High stress increases cortisol, which can worsen acne. Try exercise, meditation, or better sleep habits.  
- **Hygiene** – Change pillowcases and towels frequently, avoid touching your face, and clean your phone screen regularly.  

### **3. Medical Treatments (If Needed)**  
- **Prescription Topicals** – If OTC treatments don’t work, consult a dermatologist for stronger options like tretinoin or clindamycin.  
- **Oral Medications** – If acne is severe, your doctor might suggest antibiotics, hormonal therapy (for hormonal acne), or isotretinoin (Accutane).

Diet plays a significant role in managing acne, especially since you have oily and pimple-prone skin. Here are some **acne-friendly dietary recommendations**:  

### **Foods to Include**  
#### **1. Anti-Inflammatory Foods**  
Acne is linked to inflammation, so eating anti-inflammatory foods can help:  
- **Fatty fish** (salmon, sardines, mackerel) – Rich in omega-3s, which reduce inflammation.  
- **Nuts & seeds** (walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseeds) – Provide omega-3s and zinc.  
- **Leafy greens** (spinach, kale, arugula) – High in antioxidants and vitamins.  
- **Berries** (blueberries, strawberries, blackberries) – Reduce oxidative stress and inflammation.  

#### **2. Foods That Regulate Blood Sugar**  
High glycemic index (GI) foods spike insulin levels, increasing oil production and worsening acne. Choose **low-GI foods**:  
- **Whole grains** (quinoa, brown rice, oats) instead of white rice and bread.  
- **Legumes** (lentils, chickpeas, black beans).  
- **Sweet potatoes** instead of white potatoes.  

#### **3. Gut-Healthy Foods**  
A healthy gut reduces inflammation and improves skin health:  
- **Probiotics** (yogurt, kimchi, sauerkraut, kefir) – Improve gut bacteria.  
- **Prebiotics** (garlic, onions, bananas, asparagus) – Feed good gut bacteria.  

#### **4. Zinc-Rich Foods**  
Zinc helps control oil production and reduces inflammation:  
- **Pumpkin seeds, oysters, beef, eggs, chickpeas.**  

#### **5. Vitamin A & E Foods**  
Vitamin A helps regulate oil production, and vitamin E protects the skin:  
- **Carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach** (vitamin A).  
- **Almonds, sunflower seeds, avocados** (vitamin E).  

### **Foods to Avoid or Limit**  
#### **1. High-Glycemic Foods**  
These cause insulin spikes, leading to more oil production:  
- White bread, white rice, pastries, sugary cereals.  
- Soft drinks, fruit juices (stick to whole fruits instead).  
- Processed snacks (chips, cookies, crackers).  

#### **2. Dairy (Especially Milk)**  
- Dairy can increase insulin-like growth factor (IGF-1), worsening acne.  
- Some people see improvement by reducing **cow’s milk** (try almond or oat milk instead).  
- **Fermented dairy (yogurt, kefir)** may be better than regular milk.  

#### **3. Fried & Fast Foods**  
- High in unhealthy fats and oils that trigger inflammation.  
- Reduce deep-fried foods, fast food, and processed meats.  

#### **4. Excessive Sugar**  
- Sugary foods can increase oil production and inflammation.  
- Cut back on candy, sodas, ice cream, and processed desserts.  

#### **5. Whey Protein Supplements**  
- Whey protein can trigger acne in some people. Try **plant-based protein (pea or hemp protein)** instead.  

### **Hydration Tips**  
- Drink **at least 2-3 liters of water daily** to flush out toxins.  
- Green tea is great for reducing inflammation and contains antioxidants that benefit the skin.  
- Lemon water can help with digestion and detoxification.
