To air fry chicken wings so they turn out crispy and juicy, use the following general settings:

### **Air Fryer Settings for Chicken Wings:**

- **Temperature:** 200°C (390–400°F)  
- **Time:** 20–25 minutes  
- **Flip/Wiggle:** Shake or flip halfway through (around the 10–12 minute mark)

### **Tips:**

1. **Preheat the air fryer** for 2–3 minutes for more even cooking.
2. **Pat wings dry** before seasoning to ensure crispiness.
3. **Avoid overcrowding** the basket — cook in batches if needed.
4. Optionally, **toss with baking powder** (not baking soda) before cooking to help the skin crisp up.

Great! Since the wings are marinated, here’s how to air fry them properly:

### **Air Frying Marinated Chicken Wings**

- **Temperature:** 190–200°C (375–400°F)  
- **Time:** 22–26 minutes  
- **Flip halfway:** At around 12–13 minutes

### **Tips for Marinated Wings:**

1. **Drain off excess marinade** before air frying to avoid too much moisture in the basket (which can prevent crisping).
2. **Lightly oil the basket** to prevent sticking.
3. If the marinade contains sugar (like honey or BBQ), reduce the temperature slightly (e.g. 190°C) to avoid burning.
4. After air frying, **let them rest for 2–3 minutes** so juices redistribute.
