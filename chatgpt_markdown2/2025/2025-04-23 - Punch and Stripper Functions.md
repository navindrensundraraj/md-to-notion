# Punch and Stripper Functions

---
**User:**

What's the meaning of punch and stripper in a blanking dieset for semiconductor

---
**Assistant:**

In a **blanking dieset** used for **semiconductor packaging**, the terms **punch** and **stripper** refer to specific components of the die that work together to cut (or "blank") materials like lead frames, films, or substrates.

### 1. **Punch**
- The **punch** is the tool component that moves downward into the material.
- Its function is to **shear or cut out a specific shape** from the material, typically by pushing it through a die cavity.
- In semiconductor packaging, this could be punching out parts of a lead frame or cutting polyimide tape, for example.
- It must be **precisely aligned** and **sharply machined** to ensure clean cuts and minimal material deformation.

### 2. **Stripper**
- The **stripper** is a component that **holds the material in place** during the punching process.
- It **prevents the punched material (or scrap)** from sticking to the punch as it retracts.
- The stripper may also **strip the material off the punch** to reset for the next cycle.
- It ensures clean separation between the punch and the workpiece, avoiding jams and maintaining tool life.

### In Summary:
- **Punch** = the cutting tool that moves down.
- **Stripper** = the support piece that holds material and separates punch from workpiece.
