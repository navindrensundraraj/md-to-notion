Automate request money on monthly in Malaysia

**Assistant:**

In Malaysia, you can automate monthly **money requests** using several methods, depending on the platform or bank you prefer. Here are some options:

### **1. Bank Standing Instructions (Auto-Debit)**
   - Most Malaysian banks allow you to set up a standing instruction (SI) to automatically deduct a specific amount from the payer's account and transfer it to yours.
   - **How to set up:**
     - Log in to your online banking.
     - Look for **Standing Instructions / Auto-Debit**.
     - Enter the payer’s account details and set the frequency (monthly).
     - Confirm and submit.

### **2. DuitNow Request (Maybank, CIMB, Public Bank, etc.)**
   - DuitNow Request allows you to send an automated request for money from another person via their phone number or account number.
   - **How to use:**
     - Open your mobile banking app (Maybank2U, CIMB Clicks, etc.).
     - Go to **DuitNow > Request Money**.
     - Enter the recipient's details and amount.
     - Choose **recurring request** (if supported).
     - Submit.

### **3. eWallets (Touch ‘n Go, GrabPay, Boost)**
   - Some eWallets allow you to send **automated payment requests**.
   - **Example (Touch ‘n Go eWallet):**
     - Go to **Pay & Request**.
     - Enter recipient’s phone number.
     - Set the request amount.
     - If the app supports scheduling, set it to **monthly**.

### **4. Payment Apps (PayPal, Wise, Revolut)**
   - If you deal with international payments, apps like **PayPal** allow you to set up recurring invoices or money requests.

### **5. Third-Party Apps (Splitwise, Billplz, SenangPay)**
   - **Splitwise**: Helps track and remind people to pay shared bills.
   - **Billplz / SenangPay**: Lets businesses or individuals create automated payment requests.
