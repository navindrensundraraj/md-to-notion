Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) symptoms can vary depending on the severity and location of the infection (bladder, urethra, or kidneys). Common symptoms include:  

### **General UTI Symptoms**  
- Frequent urge to urinate  
- Burning sensation or pain during urination  
- Cloudy, dark, or strong-smelling urine  
- Passing small amounts of urine frequently  
- Lower abdominal pain or pressure  

### **Bladder Infection (Cystitis) Symptoms**  
- Pelvic pain (especially in women)  
- Blood in urine (hematuria)  
- Feeling of incomplete bladder emptying  

### **Urethra Infection (Urethritis) Symptoms**  
- Burning sensation during urination  
- Discharge from the urethra (more common in men)  

### **Kidney Infection (Pyelonephritis) Symptoms** (more severe)  
- Fever and chills  
- Flank or lower back pain  
- Nausea or vomiting  
- Fatigue or weakness  

If you suspect a UTI, it’s best to see a doctor for a urine test and antibiotics if needed. Drinking plenty of water and urinating frequently can help flush out bacteria.
