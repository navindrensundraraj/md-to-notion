# Reasons for Period Delay

**Assistant:**

There are actually quite a few reasons for period delays besides pregnancy. Some common ones are:

- **Stress**: High stress levels can mess with your hormones and delay ovulation.
- **Weight changes**: Sudden weight loss, gain, or very low body fat can disrupt your cycle.
- **Excessive exercise**: Overtraining, especially with low body fat, can delay or stop periods.
- **Hormonal imbalances**: Conditions like PCOS (polycystic ovary syndrome) often cause irregular periods.
- **Thyroid problems**: Both overactive and underactive thyroid glands can affect your cycle.
- **Illness**: Being sick or having a chronic condition can throw off your hormones temporarily.
- **Medications**: Some meds, like antidepressants or chemotherapy drugs, can cause irregularities.
- **Travel**: Especially crossing time zones, it can affect your internal clock and cycle.
- **Perimenopause**: If you're in your late 30s to 40s, your cycle might naturally become irregular.
- **Breastfeeding**: Hormones involved in milk production can suppress your period.
- **Birth control**: Starting, stopping, or missing pills can all affect timing.

If your period is more than 1–2 weeks late and you're sure you're not pregnant, it might be good to check in with a doctor just to rule out anything serious.

**Assistant:**

In the first month of pregnancy, symptoms can be pretty subtle — and they can feel a lot like PMS. Common early signs include:

- **Missed period** (the biggest clue)
- **Fatigue** (feeling really tired even after normal rest)
- **Breast changes** (tender, swollen, or sensitive breasts)
- **Nausea** (sometimes called "morning sickness," but it can happen any time)
- **Frequent urination** (feeling like you need to pee more often)
- **Mood swings** (due to hormone changes)
- **Mild cramping** (similar to period cramps) and **spotting** (called implantation bleeding)
- **Food cravings or aversions** (suddenly wanting or disliking certain foods)
- **Bloating** (feeling puffy or gassy)
- **Light-headedness or dizziness**

Not everyone gets all of these symptoms, and for some people, the first month might not feel much different at all.

**Assistant:**

Yes, it definitely can.  
Taking a **morning-after pill** (like Plan B, Postinor, etc.) can **disrupt your menstrual cycle** because it gives you a big dose of hormones (usually levonorgestrel or ulipristal acetate).  

It can cause:
- **Early period** (your next period comes sooner)
- **Delayed period** (sometimes by up to **1–2 weeks**)
- **Heavier or lighter bleeding** than usual
- **Spotting** between periods
- **Changes in flow or cramping**

If your period is **more than 3 weeks late** after taking the morning-after pill, it's a good idea to take a **pregnancy test** just to be sure, even if it's unlikely.
