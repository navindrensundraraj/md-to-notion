# Mold Release Film in QFN

---
**User:**

What is the use of Mold Release Film on lead/DAP of QFN packages?

---
**Assistant:**

Mold Release Film is used in the **molding process** of semiconductor packaging, especially for **QFN (Quad Flat No-Lead)** packages, including **leadframe-based or DAP (Die Attach Paddle)** types. Its main purpose is to **prevent the molded epoxy compound (EMC)** from sticking to certain parts of the leadframe or molding tools.

### Specifically, the Mold Release Film is used on the **lead and DAP area** of QFN packages to:

---

### 🔹 **1. Prevent Epoxy Bleed onto Leads/DAP**
- During molding, epoxy can flow or "bleed" over areas it shouldn't—especially the **leads (I/O contacts)** and the **DAP**, which are critical for electrical and thermal performance.
- The mold release film **acts as a barrier**, preventing epoxy from contaminating these areas.

---

### 🔹 **2. Improve Lead/DAP Surface Cleanliness for Plating**
- Post-molding, leads and DAP are often subjected to **plating processes** (like NiPdAu or Ag) or soldering.
- If there’s epoxy residue, it causes **plating defects** or **solderability issues**.
- The film helps ensure these surfaces are **clean and free of mold compound**, improving downstream yield.

---

### 🔹 **3. Enhance Mold Tool Life and Maintenance**
- The film also reduces epoxy buildup on the **mold cavity surfaces**, especially around critical leadframe features.
- This reduces the frequency of **tool cleaning/maintenance** and increases tool **lifespan**.

---

### 🔹 **4. Control Package Warpage or Flash**
- Proper use of mold release film helps **reduce flash** (excess resin that spreads outside mold cavity).
- It also helps maintain package coplanarity and structural integrity post-molding.

---

### Summary Table:

| Purpose                         | Benefit                                           |
|---------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Prevents epoxy bleed            | Clean leads and DAP for good electrical contact   |
| Maintains plating quality       | Improves solderability and electrical connection  |
| Reduces mold tool contamination | Less downtime, longer tool life                   |
| Controls flash/warpage          | Ensures package quality and dimensional control   |

---
