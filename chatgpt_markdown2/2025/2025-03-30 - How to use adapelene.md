Adapalene is a topical retinoid primarily used to treat acne. Here's how to use it properly:  

### **How to Apply Adapalene**  
1. **Wash Your Face:**  
   - Use a mild cleanser and pat your skin dry. Avoid scrubbing.  

2. **Apply a Pea-Sized Amount:**  
   - Take a small amount (about a pea-sized drop) and apply a thin layer evenly over the affected areas.  
   - Avoid the eyes, lips, and nostrils.  

3. **Use at Night:**  
   - Apply adapalene once daily at night before bed.  
   - Sun exposure can make your skin more sensitive, so using it at night minimizes irritation.  

4. **Moisturize:**  
   - Since adapalene can cause dryness, use a non-comedogenic moisturizer to prevent irritation.  

5. **Use Sunscreen in the Morning:**  
   - Your skin will be more sensitive to UV rays, so apply sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher) daily.  

### **What to Expect**  
- **Initial Irritation (First 2–4 weeks):** Some redness, peeling, or mild burning is normal.  
- **Purging Phase:** Acne may worsen before it gets better. Stick with it!  
- **Results:** Typically, noticeable improvement occurs after 8–12 weeks.  

### **What to Avoid**  
- Harsh skincare products (scrubs, benzoyl peroxide, alcohol-based toners).  
- Waxing or exfoliating frequently (it can cause excessive irritation).  
- Picking at acne (this can worsen scarring).
