# Rubber Band in Oil

---
**User:**

I accidentally dropped a rubber band in hot oil while cooking

---
**Assistant:**

Dropping a **rubber band into hot oil** can be dangerous for a few reasons. Here's what you should know and do:

---

### 🔥 What Happens When a Rubber Band Falls into Hot Oil:
1. **Melting or Burning**: Rubber can **melt**, **burn**, or even **release toxic fumes** (especially if it contains synthetic compounds like latex or silicone).
2. **Chemical Contamination**: Heated rubber can **leach harmful chemicals** into the oil — not safe to ingest.
3. **Fire Hazard**: If the oil is extremely hot, it may **ignite** or cause a **flare-up**.

---

### ✅ What You Should Do:
- **Turn off the heat immediately.**
- **Carefully remove the rubber band** (use tongs if it hasn't disintegrated).
- **Do NOT reuse the oil** – discard it safely once it has cooled down.
- **Ventilate your kitchen** (open windows, turn on an exhaust fan).
- If there's a strong plastic/rubber smell or smoke, **leave the room briefly** and **avoid inhaling** the fumes.

---

### 🚫 Do NOT:
- Try to scoop it out while the oil is extremely hot.
- Taste or use the oil/food that was cooking.

---

### Should You Be Worried?
If you didn’t eat any of the food or oil contaminated by the rubber band, **you’re fine** — just clean up and cook a new batch.

If you **did eat** some of the food before noticing, you’ll likely be okay, but:
- Monitor for symptoms like **nausea, headache, or throat irritation**.
- If you feel unwell, seek **medical advice**, just to be safe.
