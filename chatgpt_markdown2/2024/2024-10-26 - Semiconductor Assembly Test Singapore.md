Singapore is home to a well-established semiconductor industry, including assembly and test sites operated by major global players. These facilities play a crucial role in the backend of semiconductor manufacturing, which includes assembly (packaging the silicon chips) and testing (ensuring functionality and performance). Some prominent companies with semiconductor assembly and test facilities in Singapore include:

1. **Micron Technology** – Specializes in memory and storage solutions and has a strong presence in Singapore for assembly, testing, and even R&D.
2. **STMicroelectronics** – Operates a large facility for semiconductor assembly and testing, with a focus on automotive, industrial, and consumer applications.
3. **Infineon Technologies** – Has a regional hub in Singapore and runs facilities for assembly and testing of its power and automotive components.
4. **GlobalFoundries** – Operates multiple plants in Singapore for semiconductor assembly, testing, and fabrication.
5. **Amkor Technology** – Known for contract semiconductor assembly and test services, with a significant presence in Asia, including Singapore.
6. **Texas Instruments** – Runs assembly and test operations in Singapore, specializing in analog and embedded processing products.

These sites are vital in Singapore's semiconductor supply chain, complementing the country's wafer fabrication capabilities and supporting its reputation as a regional hub for high-tech manufacturing and innovation.

The majority of semiconductor assembly and test operations in Southeast Asia are centered in Malaysia, where many global players have extensive facilities. Key companies, such as ASE Malaysia, Carsem, and UTAC, provide comprehensive assembly and testing services, particularly in Penang and Kuala Lumpur, where they support automotive, power, and consumer electronics industries with advanced packaging and testing capabilities.

Singapore, while also active in the semiconductor industry, tends to focus on higher-value sectors like research and development, high-tech manufacturing, and testing for advanced applications. For instance, UTAC, a significant player, recently expanded its test and assembly capabilities in Singapore, focusing on high-complexity semiconductor solutions.

Thus, while both countries contribute to the semiconductor sector, Malaysia is more prominent as a regional hub specifically for assembly and test sites, whereas Singapore concentrates on high-tech manufacturing and R&D initiatives within the sector【8†source】【9†source】【11†source】.
