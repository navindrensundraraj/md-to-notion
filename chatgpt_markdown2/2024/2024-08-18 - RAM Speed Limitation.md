# RAM Speed Limitation

---
**User:**

My ram is 3200mhz but msi gf63 thin 2019 laptop only reads up to 2666mhz

---
**Assistant:**

The user has an MSI GF63 Thin 2019 laptop with 3200MHz RAM, but the laptop only reads up to 2666MHz.

---
**Tool:**

Model set context updated.

---
**Assistant:**

Your MSI GF63 Thin 2019 laptop has a RAM speed limit of 2666MHz due to its motherboard and processor constraints. Even though your RAM is capable of 3200MHz, the laptop's hardware will automatically downclock it to 2666MHz, which is the maximum speed supported by the system. Unfortunately, this is a hardware limitation that cannot be changed.

---
**User:**

How to know my laptop max ram speed

---
**Assistant:**

To find out your laptop's maximum supported RAM speed, you can use the following methods:

### 1. **Check the Laptop's Specifications:**
   - **Manufacturer's Website:** Visit MSI's official website and look up your laptop model (MSI GF63 Thin 2019). The specifications page should list the maximum RAM speed supported.
   - **User Manual:** The user manual that came with your laptop or a downloadable version from MSI's site should also have this information.

### 2. **Use System Information Tools:**
   - **CPU-Z:** This free tool provides detailed information about your laptop's CPU, motherboard, and RAM. 
     - Download and run CPU-Z.
     - Check the "Memory" tab to see the current RAM speed.
     - The "SPD" tab will list the maximum speed supported by the installed RAM modules. However, to check the motherboard's max supported speed, you'll need to cross-reference with your CPU model or motherboard specs.
   - **HWiNFO:** Another tool that gives detailed system information.
     - Download and run HWiNFO.
     - In the "Memory" section, you'll see the current speed and the maximum supported speed according to the system's configuration.

### 3. **BIOS/UEFI:**
   - Restart your laptop and enter the BIOS/UEFI settings (usually by pressing `DEL`, `F2`, or a similar key during boot).
   - Look for the "Memory" or "RAM" section. It might show the supported speeds.

### 4. **MSI Support:**
   - Contact MSI customer support or visit their support forums. They can provide exact details about your laptop's memory compatibility.

By combining these methods, you should be able to determine the maximum RAM speed your laptop can support.
