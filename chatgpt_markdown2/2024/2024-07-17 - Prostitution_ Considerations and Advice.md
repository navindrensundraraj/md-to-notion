1. **Protection Against STIs**: Condoms are particularly effective against STIs that are transmitted through bodily fluids, such as HIV, gonorrhea, and chlamydia. They provide a physical barrier that prevents the exchange of these fluids.
2. **Skin-to-Skin STIs**: For STIs that are transmitted through skin-to-skin contact, such as herpes, human papillomavirus (HPV), and syphilis, condoms reduce the risk but do not eliminate it entirely. These infections can affect areas not covered by a condom.

While condoms significantly reduce the risk of many STIs, no method is 100% effective. Combining condom use with regular STI testing, communication with partners, and other safe sex practices can further enhance protection.
