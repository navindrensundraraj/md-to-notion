# North Facing LED Issues

---
**User:**

Does north facing LED cause interference with OEM keycaps

---
**Assistant:**

North-facing LEDs on keyboards can cause interference with certain OEM keycaps, especially those with shorter profiles or non-standard stem placements. This interference happens when the LED positioning at the top (north) of the switch base affects the fit or feel of the keycap on the switch stem, sometimes leading to less stable keypresses or altered sound.

Many OEM keycaps are designed for south-facing LEDs, where the light shines from the bottom of the switch, which allows for better compatibility. The issue is most noticeable with Cherry profile keycaps, which can hit or rub against the switch housing in north-facing configurations, potentially affecting the typing experience.

If you’re using OEM keycaps and experiencing this issue, switching to a different keycap profile (such as SA, DSA, or XDA) or using switches designed to avoid this interference can help.

Can you do a side by side comparison for north and south facing LEDs

---
**Assistant:**

Here’s a side-by-side comparison of north-facing and south-facing LEDs on mechanical keyboards:

| **Feature**                  | **North-Facing LEDs**                         | **South-Facing LEDs**                          |
|------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| **LED Position**              | LED is positioned above the switch stem (north side of the switch). | LED is positioned below the switch stem (south side of the switch). |
| **Keycap Interference**       | Can cause interference with certain keycap profiles, especially Cherry profile or OEM keycaps. | Typically no interference with most keycap profiles, including Cherry and OEM keycaps. |
| **RGB Illumination**          | Better for illuminating translucent legends on north-facing keycaps (legends on the top of the key). | Better for illuminating legends on south-facing keycaps (legends on the bottom of the key). |
| **Common Keycap Compatibility Issues** | Common with Cherry profile keycaps due to shorter design and interference with switch housing. | Generally compatible with most keycaps, including Cherry profile. |
| **Preferred for Custom Builds** | Less common in custom builds due to interference issues. | More common in custom builds to avoid keycap interference issues. |
| **Typing Feel**               | May affect typing feel if keycaps rub against switch housing. | Generally smoother typing feel with no interference. |
| **Sound Impact**              | Keycap interference may also alter the sound, especially with Cherry profile keycaps. | Typically no sound issues related to keycap interference. |

### In summary:
- **North-facing LEDs** are better for lighting up keycaps with legends at the top but may cause interference with certain keycaps.
- **South-facing LEDs** avoid keycap interference and are generally more compatible with various keycap profiles, especially custom and Cherry profiles, though they may not align well with top-legend lighting.

Which setup works best for you depends on your keycap preferences and whether you're prioritizing lighting or smooth typing performance.

Here’s a side-by-side comparison of **shine-through keycaps** and **normal keycaps** (non-shine-through keycaps):

| **Feature**               | **Shine-Through Keycaps**                           | **Normal Keycaps**                               |
|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| **Material**               | Usually made from ABS or PBT plastic with transparent legends or translucent areas to let RGB lighting pass through. | Typically made from ABS, PBT, or other materials, without translucent or transparent legends. |
| **Legend Visibility**      | Legends are illuminated from behind, making them visible in both well-lit and low-light environments. | Legends are not illuminated and rely on ambient lighting to be visible. |
| **RGB/Backlight Effect**   | Designed to showcase RGB/backlighting, with the light shining through the keycap legends or surrounding areas. | RGB/backlighting only highlights the base of the keycap or the switch, without shining through the legend. |
| **Aesthetic**              | Offers a more vibrant, dynamic look, especially in low light. The backlight enhances the keyboard’s appearance. | Has a more traditional or professional aesthetic, often preferred for minimal or non-gamer setups. |
| **Durability**             | ABS shine-through keycaps can wear down and develop a greasy shine faster. PBT shine-through keycaps are more durable. | PBT keycaps tend to be more durable and resist wear, while ABS keycaps may develop shine over time. |
| **Typing Experience**      | Typing experience is largely similar, but may feel smoother or more defined if PBT is used. ABS shine-through keycaps can feel thinner or less premium. | High-quality PBT keycaps provide a more satisfying, textured feel. ABS keycaps may feel smoother or thinner over time. |
| **Customization**          | Widely used in gaming setups, as the shine-through effects can be customized with different RGB lighting effects. | Better suited for enthusiasts who prefer unique designs, textures, and colors that don’t rely on RGB or lighting effects. |

### Summary:
- **Shine-through keycaps** are ideal for those who want to emphasize RGB lighting and prefer a more vibrant look, but they may wear out faster depending on the material (especially ABS).
- **Normal keycaps** offer a more classic look, come in more design varieties, and are generally more durable, particularly when made from PBT.

If you’re into RGB lighting, shine-through keycaps are great, but for a more solid typing experience and long-term durability, normal PBT keycaps might be better.
