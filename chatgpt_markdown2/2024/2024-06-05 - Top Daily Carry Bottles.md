Plastic bottles can handle hot water to varying degrees depending on the type of plastic they are made from. Here are some common types of plastic used in water bottles and their suitability for hot water:

1. **Tritan (used in Nalgene bottles)**:
   - **Heat Resistance**: Can typically withstand temperatures up to about 100°C (212°F), which is the boiling point of water. However, it's generally recommended to avoid pouring boiling water directly into these bottles to prolong their lifespan and avoid warping.
   - **Safety**: BPA-free, durable, and doesn't retain odors or flavors.

2. **Polypropylene (used in some CamelBak bottles)**:
   - **Heat Resistance**: Can generally handle temperatures up to about 100°C (212°F), but prolonged exposure to boiling water is not recommended.
   - **Safety**: BPA-free, commonly used for food and beverage containers.

3. **Low-Density Polyethylene (LDPE)**:
   - **Heat Resistance**: Can handle temperatures up to about 80°C (176°F).
   - **Safety**: BPA-free, but not as durable or heat-resistant as Tritan or polypropylene.

4. **High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE)**:
   - **Heat Resistance**: Can typically withstand temperatures up to about 120°C (248°F).
   - **Safety**: BPA-free, durable, and often used in milk jugs and other food containers.

### General Tips:

- **Always check the manufacturer's recommendations** for specific temperature limits and usage guidelines for your bottle.

If hot water usage is a frequent need, opting for a stainless steel bottle might be a more durable and safe choice.
