# Copper Leadframe Color Change

---
**User:**

Can copper leadframe change color from brown to silver upon after 175c 5 hrs curing

---
**Assistant:**

Yes, a copper leadframe can change color from brown to silver after being subjected to curing at 175°C for 5 hours, depending on the specific conditions. Here's why:

1. **Oxidation and Reduction:**  
   - **Before curing:** The brown color on the copper surface could be due to oxidation (copper oxide, CuO, or Cu₂O) formed during previous processes or storage.
   - **During curing:** If the curing environment contains a reducing atmosphere (e.g., hydrogen gas or certain chemicals in the resin), the oxides can reduce back to metallic copper, resulting in a silvery appearance.

2. **Resin Interaction:**  
   - If a resin or coating is applied during curing, it can react with the copper surface, altering its appearance. The resin might chemically interact with the oxide layer, leaving a shiny surface.

3. **Thermal Annealing:**  
   - At elevated temperatures, the surface of the copper can undergo changes. Oxides may become more uniform, and the appearance might shift to a more metallic or silvery color.

4. **Contaminants or Residues Removal:**  
   - If curing involves exposure to solvents, gases, or vacuum, it might clean the surface, removing any tarnish or contaminants that cause a brownish color.

If you're analyzing this change for quality or process control, consider factors such as:
- The curing environment (air, vacuum, or inert gas).  
- Any pre-treatments applied to the leadframe.  
- The composition of the resin or adhesive used in the process.
