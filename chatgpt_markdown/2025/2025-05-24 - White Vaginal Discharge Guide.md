---

White vaginal discharge is common and often completely normal. However, its meaning can vary depending on its **appearance, smell, and any associated symptoms**. Here’s a quick guide:

### **Normal White Discharge**
- **Thick or creamy white** (like lotion or milk)
- **No foul odor**
- Occurs **before or after your period**, or during **ovulation**
- **No itching, burning, or pain**

This is usually just your body’s way of cleaning and maintaining vaginal health.

---

### **When White Discharge Might Be a Concern**
1. **Yeast Infection**  
   - Thick, white, **cottage cheese-like** discharge  
   - **Itching, redness, and burning** around the vulva  
   - Caused by an overgrowth of *Candida* yeast

2. **Bacterial Vaginosis (BV)**  
   - White or grayish discharge  
   - **Strong fishy odor**  
   - Possibly thin and watery  
   - Caused by an imbalance in vaginal bacteria

3. **Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)**  
   - May cause abnormal discharge, pain, or odor  
   - Examples: **chlamydia**, **gonorrhea**, **trichomoniasis**

---

### **When to See a Doctor**
- Persistent or **unusual discharge**
- **Bad odor**, especially fishy
- **Itching**, burning, or swelling
- Pain during urination or sex

If you're unsure, it's best to get it checked by a healthcare provider. They can do a quick test to confirm if it's an infection and prescribe treatment if needed.

Here’s why:

### 1. **If the burning is due to an infection (like a yeast infection or bacterial vaginosis)**:
- Oral sex can **introduce more bacteria or yeast**, which may worsen the infection.

### 2. **If it's due to dryness, irritation, or allergic reaction**:
- Saliva can temporarily lubricate, but it can also **dry out** and irritate the area afterward.
- Any perfumes, food particles, or chemicals in the mouth can **aggravate the irritation**.

---

### Best Approach for Burning Sensation:
- **Identify the cause** (infection, irritation, hormone-related, etc.)
- Use **soothing treatments** (like plain water rinses, unscented moisturizers, or prescribed meds)
