# High Creatinine Levels Causes

High creatinine levels usually indicate that your kidneys are not functioning properly, since creatinine is a waste product filtered out of your blood by the kidneys. Here’s a quick overview:

### **Possible Causes of High Creatinine:**
1. **Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)** – A progressive loss of kidney function.
2. **Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)** – Sudden damage due to dehydration, infection, medications, or blockages.
3. **High Protein Intake or Supplements** – Diet high in protein or creatine supplements.
4. **Muscle Injury** – Since creatinine comes from muscle metabolism, severe muscle breakdown can raise levels.
5. **Medications** – NSAIDs, antibiotics (like vancomycin), ACE inhibitors, etc.
6. **Dehydration** – Can falsely elevate creatinine levels.

### **Symptoms to Watch For:**
- Fatigue
- Swelling in legs or face
- Decreased urine output
- Shortness of breath
- Confusion (in later stages)

### **What You Can Do:**
- **Hydrate adequately**, unless advised otherwise.
- **Avoid nephrotoxic medications/supplements**.
- **Limit protein** if advised by your doctor.
- **Follow up with a nephrologist** (kidney specialist).
- **Check eGFR and other kidney function tests** to assess the extent.
