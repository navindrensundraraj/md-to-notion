# Kashmiri vs Baba's Chilli

---
**User:**

Kashmiri chilli powder vs Baba's chilli powder

---
**Assistant:**

**Kashmiri chilli powder** and **Baba's chilli powder** differ in taste, color, heat level, and usage. Here's a detailed comparison:

---

### 🔥 **1. Heat (Spiciness)**
- **Kashmiri Chilli Powder**: Mild heat (Scoville rating around 1,000–2,000). Ideal if you want vibrant red color without too much spiciness.
- **Baba's Chilli Powder**: Medium to high heat, depending on the batch. Spicier than Kashmiri chilli powder.

---

### 🎨 **2. Color**
- **Kashmiri Chilli Powder**: Bright, deep red hue. Often used for color in dishes like tandoori, curries, biryanis.
- **Baba's Chilli Powder**: Darker red, less vibrant than Kashmiri. Prioritizes heat over color.

---

### 🍛 **3. Flavor Profile**
- **Kashmiri Chilli Powder**: Slightly smoky and fruity; great for layered flavors.
- **Baba's Chilli Powder**: Pure, straightforward heat with minimal complexity.

---

### 🌶️ **4. Culinary Use**
- **Kashmiri**: Used in North Indian and Mughlai dishes for color and mild spice. Common in butter chicken, rogan josh.
- **Baba's**: Commonly used in Malaysian and South Indian cooking where more heat is desired — nasi lemak sambal, kari ayam, etc.

---

### 🏷️ **5. Brand vs. Type**
- **Kashmiri**: A type of chilli powder (can be from various brands).
- **Baba's**: A **brand** of chilli powder (Malaysian), using a mix of dried chillies — not Kashmiri. More for general use.

---

### ✅ **Conclusion**
| Feature              | Kashmiri Chilli Powder         | Baba's Chilli Powder            |
|----------------------|-------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Heat level           | Mild                          | Medium to Hot                   |
| Color                | Bright red                    | Dull to deep red                |
| Flavor               | Mild, fruity                  | Strong, pungent                 |
| Best for             | Color, mild dishes            | Spicy Malaysian/Indian dishes   |
| Availability         | Indian stores                 | Supermarkets in Malaysia        |

---

If you're cooking local Malaysian dishes, **Baba’s is convenient and spicy**. If you want more **color with less spice**, especially for Indian or fusion dishes, **go for Kashmiri chilli powder**. Some chefs even use **both together** for the perfect balance.
