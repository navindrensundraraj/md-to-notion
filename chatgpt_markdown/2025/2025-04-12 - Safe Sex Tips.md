Having safe sex means protecting yourself and your partner(s) from sexually transmitted infections (STIs) and unintended pregnancies. Here are the key ways to practice safe sex:

---

### 1. **Use Condoms Correctly (Male or Female)**
- **Male condoms** (latex or polyurethane) are worn on the penis.
- **Female/internal condoms** are inserted into the vagina.
- Use a new condom every time you have sex (vaginal, anal, or oral).
- Don’t use oil-based lubricants with latex condoms (they can break).

---

### 2. **Consider Other Barrier Methods**
- **Dental dams** (thin latex or silicone sheets) for oral sex on a vulva or anus.
- These reduce the risk of STI transmission during oral sex.

---

### 3. **Get Regular STI Testing**
- Especially if you have multiple partners or a new partner.
- Encourage your partner to get tested too.

---

### 4. **Limit the Number of Sexual Partners**
- The more partners you have, the higher the risk.
- Having a mutually monogamous relationship with someone who has tested negative reduces risk.

---

### 5. **Use Birth Control (If Pregnancy Is a Concern)**
- Combine condoms with birth control methods like the pill, IUD, implant, or shot for extra protection.

---

### 6. **Avoid Alcohol or Drugs Before Sex**
- Being intoxicated can lead to poor decisions, like skipping protection.

---

### 7. **Communicate with Your Partner**
- Talk about STIs, boundaries, and contraception before sex.
- Consent and comfort are part of safe sex too.

---

### 8. **Consider Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP)**
- If you're at higher risk for HIV, PrEP is a daily medication that reduces your chances of getting HIV.

### **How to Talk to Your Partner About Safe Sex**

**1. Pick the Right Moment**  
- Bring it up *before* things get sexual — maybe during a chill conversation.
- Avoid doing it *in the heat of the moment* where it might feel awkward or rushed.

**2. Be Honest but Chill**  
- Example:  
  “Hey, I like where this is going, but I think it's important we talk about protection and making sure we’re both safe.”

**3. Ask, Don’t Accuse**  
- Instead of “Have you been tested?” try:  
  “When was the last time you got tested?” or  
  “I usually get tested every 6 months, just to stay on top of things. How about you?”

**4. Set Boundaries**  
- Let them know what you’re comfortable with and what’s non-negotiable (like always using a condom).

**5. Be Open to Their Side**  
- Some people have different experiences or knowledge. It's okay to have a learning conversation together.

**STI Testing (affordable or free):**

- **Private Clinics/Labs**: Faster but more expensive. Some offer anonymous testing.
