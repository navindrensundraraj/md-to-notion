# What is Jaggery

**Assistant:**

**Jaggery** is a traditional, unrefined sugar made from the **sap or juice of sugarcane or palm trees** (like date palm or coconut palm). It's commonly used in **South Asia, Southeast Asia, Africa, and Latin America**.

### Key Features of Jaggery:
- **Color**: Ranges from golden yellow to dark brown.
- **Texture**: Can be soft, crumbly, or solid blocks.
- **Taste**: Rich, caramel-like sweetness with a deep, molasses flavor.
- **Processing**: Made by boiling the juice/sap until it solidifies, without removing molasses or refining it like white sugar.

### Types of Jaggery:
1. **Sugarcane jaggery** – most common
2. **Palm jaggery** – made from date palm or coconut palm sap, usually darker and richer

### Health Aspects:
- Contains small amounts of **minerals** like iron, magnesium, and potassium
- Sometimes seen as a "healthier" alternative to white sugar, but still high in sugar content
- Used in **Ayurveda** for aiding digestion and cleansing

### Uses:
- Sweetening desserts and drinks
- Making traditional sweets (e.g. Indian *chikki*, *pongal*, *gur roti*)
- Balancing spicy flavors in curries or chutneys
