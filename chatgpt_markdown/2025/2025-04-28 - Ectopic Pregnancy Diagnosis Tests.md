An **ectopic pregnancy** happens when a fertilized egg implants **outside the uterus**, usually in a fallopian tube. Detecting it early is important because it can be life-threatening if untreated.

**Tests for ectopic pregnancy usually include:**

1. **Urine pregnancy test**:  
   - It will usually show **positive** because the body is producing hCG (pregnancy hormone).
   - But it **cannot differentiate** between a normal and ectopic pregnancy.

2. **Blood tests (Quantitative hCG)**:  
   - Measures the exact amount of hCG.  
   - In normal pregnancies, hCG levels roughly **double every 48 hours** early on.
   - In ectopic pregnancies, **hCG often rises slower** or plateaus.

3. **Transvaginal ultrasound**:  
   - A thin probe inserted into the vagina to get a close image of the uterus, fallopian tubes, and ovaries.
   - In normal pregnancy: a **gestational sac** is seen inside the uterus by around 5-6 weeks.
   - In ectopic pregnancy: **no sac in the uterus**, but sometimes a mass or fluid (bleeding) near the tube is visible.

4. **Progesterone blood test** (sometimes):  
   - Low levels can suggest an abnormal pregnancy (but not specific for ectopic).

---

**In summary**:  
- If a pregnancy test is positive but **no gestational sac is seen** in the uterus during ultrasound and/or **hCG levels rise abnormally**, doctors suspect ectopic pregnancy.  
- **Early diagnosis** is key to avoid rupture.

### **Symptoms of Ectopic Pregnancy**
- **Sharp or stabbing abdominal/pelvic pain**, usually on one side
- **Vaginal bleeding** that's lighter or heavier than a normal period
- **Shoulder pain** (if bleeding irritates the diaphragm)
- **Weakness, dizziness, fainting** (if there’s internal bleeding from rupture)
- **Low back pain**  
- Sometimes women have **no symptoms** until the tube ruptures, which becomes an emergency.

---

### **Treatment Options**
Depends on how early it's caught and your condition:

1. **Medication (Methotrexate)**  
   - If caught early and you're stable, a shot of methotrexate can **stop the pregnancy from growing**, and the body absorbs it naturally.
   - No surgery needed.
   - Requires follow-up blood tests until hCG drops to zero.

2. **Surgery**  
   - If the tube has ruptured or methotrexate isn't an option:
   - **Laparoscopic surgery** (small incisions, camera) to remove the ectopic pregnancy.
   - Sometimes the affected fallopian tube has to be removed (salpingectomy).

---

### **Important**
If you think there's any chance of an ectopic pregnancy and have **pain, bleeding, or fainting**, **go to the hospital immediately**.  
It can **rupture** and cause **serious internal bleeding**.
