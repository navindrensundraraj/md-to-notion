# RCA Tools for MRF Cost

---
**User:**

What's the best Root cause analysis tool to justify cost reduction for Mold Release Film in semiconductor packaging

---
**Assistant:**

To justify cost reduction for **Mold Release Film (MRF)** in **semiconductor packaging**, the best Root Cause Analysis (RCA) tool depends on the *nature of the cost issue* you're trying to address — whether it's over-specification, process inefficiency, quality defects, or material wastage. Here's a breakdown:

---

### 🔧 **Best RCA Tools for Cost Reduction Justification**
#### 1. **Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa)**
- **Best for**: Identifying all possible causes related to high cost (materials, methods, people, equipment, environment).
- **Why**: Gives a structured view of contributors — e.g., over-engineered specs, poor storage handling, excessive usage due to poor lamination technique.
- **How to use**:
  - Main effect: *High cost of MRF*
  - Branches: *Material*, *Method*, *Machine*, *Manpower*, *Measurement*, *Environment*

---

#### 2. **5 Whys Analysis**
- **Best for**: Drilling down to root causes in a simple way.
- **Why**: Often, high MRF cost isn't the root — it's a *symptom* of upstream inefficiencies.
- **Example**:
  1. Why is MRF cost high? → We're using too much.
  2. Why using too much? → Poor adhesion requires multiple reapplications.
  3. Why poor adhesion? → Substrate contamination.
  4. Why contaminated? → Inadequate cleaning process.
  5. Why? → No preventive maintenance SOP.

---

#### 3. **Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule)**
- **Best for**: Identifying which root causes contribute the most to cost.
- **Why**: Allows you to prioritize which issues to tackle (e.g., if 80% of cost is due to 2 main issues like scrap or supplier premium).
- **Data Needed**: 
  - Monthly spend by category (material, process losses, quality rejects)
  - Root cause categorization of defects or yield losses involving MRF

---

#### 4. **FMEA (Failure Modes and Effects Analysis)**
- **Best for**: Justifying why certain MRF specs/processes are overkill.
- **Why**: Helps identify and rank risk levels associated with different use cases.
- **Use it to ask**: Are we using a high-performance MRF where a cheaper one will do with minimal risk?

---

#### 5. **Cost Driver Tree / Cost Breakdown Analysis**
- **Best for**: Directly quantifying where cost is being added unnecessarily.
- **Why**: Justifies cost reduction by showing exact breakdown — unit cost, yield impact, rework, scrap, etc.
- **Build a tree**:
  - MRF Total Cost
    - Raw Material Price
    - Usage per unit
    - Rework/reapplication cost
    - Scrap due to improper release

---

### 🔄 Combine Tools for Strong Justification
To present a compelling cost reduction case:
1. **Use Pareto** to find top cost issues.
2. **Use Fishbone + 5 Whys** to drill into root causes.
3. **Validate with FMEA** if you propose using lower-spec or cheaper MRF.
4. **Summarize with Cost Driver Tree** to highlight savings potential.

---

### 💡 Bonus: Include Data from Pilot Run
- If proposing a cheaper MRF or process change, run a **small trial**.
- Compare **yield, cycle time, rework rate, material usage**.
- Use this real-world data to **strengthen justification** in your RCA report.
