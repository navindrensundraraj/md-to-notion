# HA vs SA Skincare

---

Hyaluronic acid vs salicylic acid

---
**Assistant:**

**Hyaluronic acid** and **salicylic acid** are both popular skincare ingredients, but they serve very different purposes:

### 1. **Hyaluronic Acid (HA)**
- **Function:** Hydration
- **Skin Type:** All types, especially **dry**, **dehydrated**, or **sensitive** skin
- **How it works:** It's a humectant, which means it attracts water to the skin, keeping it plump and moisturized.
- **Use:** Often found in serums and moisturizers. Apply to slightly damp skin, then seal with a moisturizer.
- **Common Pairings:** Works well with most ingredients, including salicylic acid.

### 2. **Salicylic Acid (SA)**
- **Function:** Exfoliation & acne control
- **Skin Type:** Best for **oily**, **acne-prone**, or **congested** skin
- **How it works:** It’s a beta hydroxy acid (BHA) that penetrates into pores to dissolve oil and dead skin cells, helping to treat and prevent acne.
- **Use:** Found in cleansers, toners, spot treatments. Can be drying—use 2-3 times a week unless your skin tolerates it well.
- **Caution:** Can cause dryness or irritation if overused.

### Summary:
- Use **HA** for **hydration and skin barrier support**.
- Use **SA** for **unclogging pores, reducing acne and oil**.
- They **can be used together**: Apply salicylic acid first (after cleansing), then follow with hyaluronic acid to replenish moisture.
