# Money Market Fund Basics

How does money market fund investment work

A **money market fund (MMF)** is a type of mutual fund that invests in short-term, low-risk debt instruments, such as:

- Treasury bills
- Certificates of deposit (CDs)
- Commercial paper
- Repurchase agreements (repos)

### Here's how it works:

1. **You invest money** into the MMF, usually through a brokerage, bank, or investment platform.

2. **The fund pools your money** with other investors' funds and uses it to buy short-term debt instruments from governments or corporations.

3. **You earn returns** based on the interest earned from these instruments. These returns are typically paid as daily dividends, which can be automatically reinvested or withdrawn.

4. **The fund maintains a stable Net Asset Value (NAV)** — usually around $1 or RM1 per unit — so the value of your investment doesn’t fluctuate much. Instead of capital gains, you earn interest income.

5. **You can redeem your money anytime**, usually without penalties, making MMFs a **highly liquid** investment option.

### Pros:
- Low risk
- Daily liquidity
- Better returns than savings accounts
- Good for parking emergency funds or cash reserves

### Cons:
- Returns are relatively low (but better than savings accounts)
- Not insured (unless provided by a specific scheme)
- May not keep up with inflation
