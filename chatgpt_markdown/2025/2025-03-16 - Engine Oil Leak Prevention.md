### **Common Causes of Oil Leaks:**  
1. **Worn-out Gaskets & Seals** – Over time, valve cover gaskets, oil pan gaskets, and crankshaft seals can degrade and cause leaks.  
2. **Loose or Over-tightened Drain Plug** – If your oil drain plug isn't tightened properly after an oil change, it can lead to slow leaks.  
3. **Degraded O-Rings in the Oil Filter Housing** – If these O-rings wear out, they can cause minor leaks.  
4. **High Engine Temperatures** – Excessive heat can cause rubber seals to harden and crack.  
5. **Improper Oil Changes** – Using the wrong oil viscosity or low-quality oil can accelerate wear on seals.  
6. **Cracks in the Engine Block** – Rare but possible, especially if the engine has been overheated.  

### **How to Prevent Oil Leaks:**  
✅ **Use quality engine oil** and change it at the recommended interval.  
✅ **Check for leaks regularly** by inspecting the ground under your car and the engine bay.  
✅ **Replace worn-out gaskets and seals** as part of preventive maintenance.  
✅ **Ensure proper installation** of the oil filter and drain plug after every oil change.
