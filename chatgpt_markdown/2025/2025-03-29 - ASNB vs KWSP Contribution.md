# ASNB vs KWSP Contribution

---
**User:**

Invest in ASNB vs KWSP voluntary contribution

---
**Assistant:**

Both **ASNB (Amanah Saham Nasional Berhad)** and **KWSP (Employees Provident Fund) voluntary contributions** have their pros and cons. Your choice should depend on your risk tolerance, liquidity needs, and long-term financial goals.

### **ASNB Investment**
✅ **Pros:**
- **Higher potential returns** (historically 5-7% p.a. for fixed-price funds like ASB/ASM, and potentially higher for variable-price funds).
- **Liquidity** – you can withdraw anytime (for fixed-price funds).
- **No lock-in period**, so you can use the funds whenever needed.

❌ **Cons:**
- **Riskier** than KWSP, especially if you invest in variable-price ASNB funds.
- **Quota limitations** – Fixed-price funds like ASB/ASM have limited availability.
- **Dividends are not guaranteed** and depend on fund performance.

---

### **KWSP Voluntary Contribution**
✅ **Pros:**
- **Stable & guaranteed returns** (historically 5-6% p.a.).
- **Dividend is tax-free**.
- **Compounding growth for retirement** – funds in KWSP grow over time with minimal risk.
- **Can be used for tax relief** (up to RM4,000 for self-contributions).

❌ **Cons:**
- **Low liquidity** – you cannot withdraw until retirement (except for approved reasons like house purchase, education, or emergencies).
- **Limited access before 55**, making it less flexible for short-term financial needs.

---

### **Which One Should You Choose?**
- **For short-term and flexible savings** → **ASNB** is better due to liquidity.
- **For long-term, stable retirement savings** → **KWSP Voluntary Contribution** is better.
- **If you want a balance** → You can do both: KWSP for secure retirement savings and ASNB for flexible investments with potential higher returns.
