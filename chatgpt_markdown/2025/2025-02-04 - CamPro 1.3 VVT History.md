The **CamPro 1.3 VVT** engine is a variant of the **CamPro** engine family developed by **Proton** in collaboration with **Lotus Engineering**. Here’s a historical record of the engine:

### **Development & Introduction**  
- The **CamPro engine** was first introduced in **2004** by **Proton** as its first in-house engine.  
- The **1.3L CamPro VVT** (Variable Valve Timing) version was later introduced as an improvement over the original CamPro engine lineup.  
- It was developed to enhance fuel efficiency, power delivery, and meet stricter emissions regulations.

### **Engine Specifications**  
- **Displacement**: 1.3 liters (1,332 cc)  
- **Configuration**: Inline-4, DOHC, 16-valve  
- **Valve Technology**: VVT (Variable Valve Timing) for improved efficiency and performance  
- **Power Output**: Around **94 hp (70 kW) @ 5,750 rpm**  
- **Torque**: Around **120 Nm @ 4,000 rpm**  
- **Fuel System**: Multi-Point Fuel Injection (MPFI)  
- **Emission Standard**: Euro 4 compliant (later versions met Euro 5 standards)  

### **Key Milestones**  
1. **Introduction of VVT Technology**  
   - Proton introduced **VVT (Variable Valve Timing)** to improve efficiency and performance, replacing the older **CamPro IAFM (Intake Air-Fuel Module)** system.  
   - This update helped reduce fuel consumption and provided better torque at lower RPMs.  

2. **Use in Proton Models**  
   - The **1.3L CamPro VVT** engine was used in models such as:  
     - **Proton Saga (2016 - present)**  
     - **Proton Iriz (2014 - 2022)**  
     - **Proton Persona (2016 - 2019)** *(lower trim levels)*  

3. **End of Production & Successor**  
   - The **CamPro engine family** was gradually phased out in favor of Proton’s **GDI & TGDI** engines, co-developed with **Geely** after their partnership in 2017.  
   - The newer **1.3L & 1.5L DOHC VVT** engines replaced the CamPro VVT in newer Proton models like the **Proton Saga (2019 facelift)** and later versions of the **Iriz & Persona**.  

### **Common Issues & Improvements**  
- **Early CamPro engines had poor low-end torque**, but the introduction of **VVT** and tuning improvements helped solve this issue.  
- **Fuel efficiency was improved**, but still lagged behind some competitors.  
- **Reliability**: Generally durable, but some owners reported issues with **engine knocking, ECU calibration problems, and higher-than-expected fuel consumption**.  

### **Conclusion**  
The **CamPro 1.3 VVT** engine was an important step for **Proton**, showcasing its ability to develop its own engine technology. While it was eventually replaced, it served as a bridge to Proton’s modern engine lineup and improved its competitiveness in the automotive market.
