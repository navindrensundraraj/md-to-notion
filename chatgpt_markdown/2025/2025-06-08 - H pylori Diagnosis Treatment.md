Thanks for sharing. A positive *Helicobacter pylori* (H. pylori) diagnosis means that this bacteria has been detected in your digestive tract — usually your stomach. Here's what you need to know and consider:

---

### 🦠 **What is H. pylori?**
H. pylori is a spiral-shaped bacterium that can infect the stomach lining and is a common cause of:
- **Gastritis** (stomach inflammation)
- **Peptic ulcers**
- It can **increase the risk of stomach cancer** if left untreated long term.

---

### 💊 **Treatment (Standard Eradication Therapy)**
You’ll most likely be prescribed a **triple or quadruple therapy** for 10–14 days:

#### Common Regimen:
- **Two antibiotics** (e.g., Clarithromycin + Amoxicillin or Metronidazole)
- **Proton pump inhibitor (PPI)** (e.g., Omeprazole, Pantoprazole) to reduce stomach acid
- Sometimes **Bismuth subsalicylate** (Pepto-Bismol) is added

**Important:** Always complete the full course even if symptoms improve early.

---

### 🍽️ **Diet & Lifestyle Tips During Treatment**
These can help reduce symptoms and promote healing:

- **Avoid**:
  - Spicy, acidic, or fried foods
  - Caffeine and alcohol
  - Smoking (it impairs healing)
  - NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen)

- **Do**:
  - Eat small, frequent meals
  - Include **probiotics** (like yogurt with live cultures or supplements) to support gut health during antibiotics
  - Stay well-hydrated

---

### 🧪 **Follow-up**
- After finishing treatment, you’ll need a **follow-up test (usually breath or stool antigen test)** at least 4 weeks later to confirm the bacteria is gone.

---

### 🛑 When to See a Doctor Urgently
- Severe or persistent stomach pain
- Vomiting blood or black stools
- Difficulty swallowing
- Significant weight loss

Yes, it **is possible** for your girlfriend to get infected with *H. pylori* from you — but it’s **not guaranteed**. Here's what you should know:

---

### 🦠 **How H. pylori is transmitted**
H. pylori spreads mainly through:

- **Mouth-to-mouth contact** (e.g., **kissing**)
- **Oral-fecal route** (e.g., contaminated food, water, or hands)
- **Sharing utensils, toothbrushes, or drinks**

It's more common in **household settings** where close contact and shared items are frequent.

---

### 💡 So yes — if you're kissing or sharing food/drinks, **she could be at risk**.

---

### 🔬 Should your girlfriend get tested?
- If she **has symptoms** (gastric pain, bloating, nausea, etc.), **yes — testing is advised.**
- If she's **symptom-free**, routine testing isn’t always necessary. But some doctors do recommend **screening close contacts** in long-term relationships, especially in high-prevalence countries (like in parts of Asia or Latin America).

---

### ✅ How to reduce transmission risk while you’re infected
Until treatment is done and follow-up confirms you're clear:

1. Avoid **kissing**
2. Don't **share utensils, food, cups, or toothbrushes**
3. Wash your hands well, especially after using the toilet
4. Practice good **oral hygiene**

Once you're confirmed clear of the infection, normal close contact can resume safely.
