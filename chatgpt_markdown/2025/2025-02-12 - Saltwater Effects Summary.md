**Assistant:**

Putting salt into water leads to **dissolution**, where the salt (sodium chloride, NaCl) separates into sodium (Na⁺) and chloride (Cl⁻) ions. This process has several effects:  

1. **Increases Boiling Point** – Saltwater boils at a slightly higher temperature than pure water (boiling point elevation).  
2. **Lowers Freezing Point** – Adding salt lowers the freezing point of water (freezing point depression), which is why salt is used to melt ice on roads.  
3. **Conducts Electricity** – The dissolved ions allow water to conduct electricity better than pure water.  
4. **Osmosis Effect** – In biological systems, saltwater can draw water out of cells, leading to dehydration.
