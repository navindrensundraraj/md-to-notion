The **996 work culture**—working **9 AM to 9 PM, 6 days a week**—is a controversial topic, especially in tech and startup industries in countries like China. Whether it’s “worth it” depends heavily on your **personal goals**, **stage of life**, and **work environment**. Here's a breakdown:

### **When it *might* feel worth it:**
- You're young, single, and want to **gain experience fast**, **build skills**, or **climb the corporate ladder quickly**.
- The company offers **high growth**, **equity**, or potential **big rewards**.
- You're **passionate** about the work and don’t mind long hours.

### **When it's probably *not* worth it:**
- It starts **ruining your health**, relationships, or mental well-being.
- You don’t see **clear growth**, recognition, or financial compensation for the extra hours.
- It becomes a **toxic culture**, where long hours are seen as loyalty instead of productivity.
- You're in it just to survive, not thrive.

### **Hidden Costs of 996:**
- **Burnout** and possible health problems.
- **Reduced creativity** and productivity over time.
- **No time for personal growth**, hobbies, or relationships.
- It’s often **not sustainable long-term**.

### Final take:
**Short bursts** of 996 can be strategically useful if it directly contributes to a **clear, high-impact goal**—like leveling up your skills or securing a promotion. But doing it indefinitely for a mediocre paycheck, under poor leadership, or at the cost of your health? Definitely **not worth it**.

Ah, so you're *not* being explicitly told to work 996, but the workload is so heavy that you end up doing it anyway—that’s a tricky spot.

### Here’s how to think about it:

#### **1. Root Cause – Is it You, the Role, or the Company?**
- **You:** Are you overcommitting, saying yes to everything, or struggling with time management?
- **The Role:** Is the scope of your role too broad for one person? Are responsibilities creeping beyond what’s fair?
- **The Company:** Is the culture subtly rewarding overwork (e.g. only praising people who stay late)?

Understanding the root helps you decide what needs changing.

---

#### **2. Is the Overwork Paying Off?**
Ask yourself:
- Are you **growing fast**, learning skills that will **unlock better opportunities**?
- Are you **getting recognized**, promoted, or well-compensated?
- Do you have a **timeline** (e.g. "I’ll do this for a year to gain X, then shift")?

If the answer is **no**, and you’re burning out—then you’re overinvesting in a **low-return asset**.

---

#### **3. Sustainability Check:**
Even if you're okay now, can you keep this up for the next 6 months? 1 year?  
If you’re already drained, anxious, or losing motivation—it’s time to **recalibrate**.

---

### What You Can Do:
