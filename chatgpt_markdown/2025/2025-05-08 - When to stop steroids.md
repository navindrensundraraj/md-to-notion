# When to stop steroids

---

When to stop applying steroid on my skin for eczema

---
**Assistant:**

You should stop applying a topical steroid for eczema when **one or more of these apply**:

1. **The eczema has cleared up or significantly improved** — No more redness, itching, or inflammation.
2. **You've reached the prescribed duration** — Most steroids are meant for short-term use (e.g., 1–2 weeks). Prolonged use without a doctor's guidance can lead to side effects like thinning skin.
3. **You notice side effects** — Such as skin thinning, lightening, increased sensitivity, or stretch marks.
4. **Your doctor advises you to taper or stop** — In some cases, especially with medium to high-potency steroids, you might need to gradually reduce frequency instead of stopping abruptly.

**Best practice:**
- For flare-ups: Use the steroid until the skin is better (often 5–7 days), then stop or switch to maintenance care with moisturizers.
- For maintenance: Some doctors recommend “weekend therapy” (applying steroids 2 days a week) to prevent relapses — but only under supervision.
