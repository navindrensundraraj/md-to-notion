To become a **Chartered Engineer (CEng)**, you must meet specific **education**, **experience**, and **competency** requirements set by the relevant **engineering regulatory body**. Below are the general requirements, with a focus on the **UK standard** (via the **Engineering Council**), which is one of the most internationally recognized systems. Other countries have similar structures but different naming and processes.

---

## 🇬🇧 **Chartered Engineer (CEng) - UK via Engineering Council**

### ✅ **1. Educational Requirements**
You need one of the following:
- An **accredited Master’s degree** in engineering (MEng), or
- A **Bachelor’s degree (BEng)** + **Master’s-level study** (e.g., MSc), or
- Demonstration of equivalent knowledge through **individual assessment** (for non-standard qualifications or international degrees)

### ✅ **2. Work Experience**
Typically **4–8 years** of post-degree professional experience, including:
- Leading projects
- Developing solutions to complex engineering problems
- Managing teams or technical resources
- Taking responsibility for decisions with significant impact

### ✅ **3. Competence and Commitment**
You must demonstrate competence in the **UK-SPEC (UK Standard for Professional Engineering Competence)**:

| Competence Area | Description |
|-----------------|-------------|
| A | Knowledge and understanding of engineering |
| B | Design and development of processes, systems, or services |
| C | Responsibility, leadership, and management |
| D | Communication and interpersonal skills |
| E | Commitment to professional standards and ethics |

This is typically demonstrated via a **Professional Review Report** and **Professional Review Interview**.

### ✅ **4. Membership with a Professional Engineering Institution**
You must be a **member** of a recognized UK institution (e.g.:
- **IMechE** – Mechanical
- **IET** – Electrical
- **IChemE** – Chemical
- **ICE** – Civil
- **RAeS** – Aerospace)

They will assess your application and support your CEng registration.

---

## 🌏 **Outside UK: Equivalent Titles**
Other countries have similar chartering or licensing systems:
- **PE (Professional Engineer)** – USA, Canada
- **CEng (India)** – via Institution of Engineers India (IEI)
- **Ir. (Malaysia)** – via Board of Engineers Malaysia (BEM)
- **RPE (Hong Kong)**
- **Chartered Professional Engineer (CPEng)** – Australia, NZ

If you're in **Malaysia**, here's a quick note:

---

## 🇲🇾 **Malaysia – "Ir." / Chartered Engineer**
- **Bachelor’s degree** in engineering (accredited by EAC)
- **Registered Graduate Engineer** with BEM
- At least **3 years of supervised experience**
- Pass the **Professional Assessment Examination (PAE)**
- Fulfill **CPD** (Continuing Professional Development) requirements
- Can then apply for **PEng (Ir.)**
