# Mefenamic Acid Overview

Mefenamic acid is a **nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID)** used to treat **mild to moderate pain, inflammation, and menstrual cramps (dysmenorrhea).** It works by blocking the production of prostaglandins, which are chemicals responsible for pain and inflammation in the body.  

### **Uses:**
- Menstrual pain (primary dysmenorrhea)  
- Headaches and body aches  
- Toothache  
- Post-surgical pain  
- Fever reduction  

### **Dosage:**
- **Adults:** Typically **500 mg initially, then 250 mg every 6 hours** as needed.  
- **Maximum duration:** Usually not recommended for more than **7 days** due to potential side effects.  

### **Side Effects:**
- Stomach pain, nausea, vomiting  
- Heartburn, indigestion  
- Dizziness, drowsiness  
- Increased risk of stomach ulcers and bleeding (long-term use)  

### **Precautions:**
- Avoid if you have a history of **stomach ulcers, kidney disease, or heart problems.**  
- Take with food or milk to reduce stomach irritation.  
- Not recommended during late pregnancy.
