ASM, ASM2, and ASM3 refer to different **Activated Sludge Models** developed by the **International Water Association (IWA)** to simulate and optimize the biological treatment processes in wastewater treatment plants. Here's a breakdown of the differences:

---

### **1. ASM (ASM1) - Introduced in 1987**
- **Focus:** Carbon and nitrogen removal.
- **Processes modeled:**
  - Aerobic growth of heterotrophic and autotrophic organisms.
  - Anoxic growth of heterotrophs (denitrification).
  - Hydrolysis of slowly biodegradable organics.
- **Limitations:**
  - No phosphorus removal.
  - Simplified representation of microbial kinetics.

**Amanah Saham** refers to a series of unit trust investment schemes managed by **Amanah Saham Nasional Berhad (ASNB)**, a subsidiary of **Permodalan Nasional Berhad (PNB)** in Malaysia. These funds are popular for their relatively stable returns and low-risk profile.

Here’s a breakdown of the key types:

---

### **1. Fixed Price Funds**  
(Price is always RM1 per unit; capital is preserved)

- **Examples:**
  - **Amanah Saham Bumiputera (ASB)**
  - **Amanah Saham Bumiputera 2 (ASB2)**
  - **Amanah Saham Malaysia (ASM)**
  - **ASM2 Wawasan**
  - **ASM3**

- **Key Features:**
  - Annual dividend payouts.
  - Low risk, government-linked investments.
  - **Only for Malaysians**, with some restricted to **Bumiputera**.
  - Dividend rate usually ~4%–5% per annum (net of tax).

---

### **2. Variable Price Funds**  
(Price fluctuates based on market performance)

- Examples include ASN Equity series and ASN Sara series.
- More suited for medium- to high-risk investors.
- Unit prices change daily, like typical mutual funds.

---

### **ASM vs ASM2 vs ASM3**  
These are **fixed price funds** open to **all Malaysians (regardless of race)**:

| Fund | Launch Year | Target Group     | Features |
|------|-------------|------------------|----------|
| ASM  | 2000        | All Malaysians   | RM1 fixed price, dividend fund |
| ASM2 Wawasan | 1999 | All Malaysians   | Similar to ASM, capped fund size |
| ASM3 | 2001        | All Malaysians   | Similar mechanics, different allocation |

All three:
- Have **similar investment objectives**.
- Differ mainly in **fund size** and **availability of units**.

**ASM (Amanah Saham Malaysia)** and **ASM3 (Amanah Saham Malaysia 3)** are both **fixed-price unit trust funds** managed by **ASNB**, with a unit price permanently fixed at **RM1**. Here's a comparison to help you understand their differences and similarities:

---

### **Similarities:**

| Feature               | ASM & ASM3                             |
|-----------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Unit Price**        | Fixed at RM1                          |
| **Dividend**          | Paid annually, usually in April       |
| **Risk**              | Low risk                              |
| **Eligibility**       | **All Malaysians** (regardless of race) |
| **Capital Guarantee** | Not legally guaranteed, but historically stable |
| **Investment Style**  | Conservative, mostly government-linked and blue-chip assets |

---

### **Differences:**

| Feature               | **ASM**                            | **ASM3**                           |
|-----------------------|------------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| **Launched**          | April 2000                         | April 2001                         |
| **Fund Size**         | ~15 billion units                  | ~5.5 billion units                 |
| **Unit Availability** | Slightly easier to get (larger size) | Harder to get (smaller fund size) |

---

### **Which One Should You Choose?**

Honestly, **both are functionally the same** in terms of returns and risk profile. The main concern is **availability**—units are often sold out quickly, especially for ASM3 due to its smaller fund size.

If you’re trying to buy units:
- **Take whichever is available.**
- **Set up auto-invest or queue** via apps like **myASNB** or visit agents (Maybank, CIMB, RHB).
