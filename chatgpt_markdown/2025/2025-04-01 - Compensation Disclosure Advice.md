# Compensation Disclosure Advice

---
**User:**

My job application requires me to include my current job compensation in detail. Should I include them

---
**Assistant:**

It depends on the job application and the norms in your industry. Here are some considerations:  

### **When to Include Compensation Details**  
✅ If the job posting **explicitly asks** for your current salary and benefits.  
✅ If it's a **common practice** in your industry (e.g., finance, consulting).  
✅ If you're applying for a **higher-paying** position and want to justify your salary expectations.  

### **When to Avoid or Be Cautious**  
❌ If it's **not mandatory**—you can write "Will disclose upon request" instead.  
❌ If you're **negotiating for a much higher salary**—stating your current lower salary might weaken your bargaining power.  
❌ If you **signed an NDA** that restricts disclosing compensation.  

### **Alternative Approach**  
If you’re uncomfortable disclosing exact figures, you can provide a **salary range** or only mention your **expected salary** for the new role.  

Since you’re planning to move to Singapore for better pay, you might want to avoid limiting yourself based on your current Malaysian salary. Instead, you can focus on **market rates** in Singapore for your role.
