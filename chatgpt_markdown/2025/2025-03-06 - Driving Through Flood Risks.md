Driving through a flooded road can be extremely risky and may cause severe damage to your Proton Saga MC2, especially since it has a low air intake position. Here’s what you need to consider before attempting to drive through a flood:

### **1. Assess the Water Depth**  
- **Safe Limit:** If the water is deeper than **half your tire height**, avoid driving through it.  
- If water reaches the **air intake or exhaust**, your engine may stall, leading to hydro lock (which can destroy the engine).  
- If you can't see the road markings, assume the water is too deep.  

### **2. Drive Slowly and Steadily**  
- Use **1st gear (manual) or Low gear (automatic)** and maintain steady acceleration.  
- Keep your speed around **5-10 km/h** to prevent water from splashing into the engine bay.  

### **3. Avoid Stopping or Changing Gears**  
- Stopping can allow water to seep into the engine or exhaust.  
- Keep engine revs high to prevent water from entering the exhaust.  

### **4. Test Your Brakes After Exiting**  
- Water can reduce braking effectiveness. Lightly apply the brakes while moving to dry them.  

### **5. Check for Damage Afterward**  
- Look for **water in the air filter or engine oil** (milky color indicates water contamination).  
- If the car **stalls in deep water, do NOT restart**; this can cause severe engine damage. Get it towed instead.  

### **Better Safe Than Sorry**  
If the water level seems too high, **turn around and find an alternative route**. Flood damage can lead to expensive repairs, and safety should always be the priority.
