# Scratching and Eczema Risks

---
**User:**

What does scratching do to my eczema skin

---
**Assistant:**

Scratching eczema skin may feel relieving in the moment, but it can significantly worsen the condition in several ways:

### 1. **Triggers the Itch-Scratch Cycle**
- Scratching damages the skin barrier, which causes **more inflammation** and **itchiness**, leading to more scratching — a vicious cycle that’s hard to break.

### 2. **Worsens Skin Damage**
- Scratching can cause:
  - **Microtears** or open wounds in the skin
  - **Thickening of the skin** (lichenification)
  - **Increased redness and swelling**

### 3. **Increases Risk of Infection**
- Broken skin allows **bacteria**, **viruses**, or **fungi** to enter, leading to infections like:
  - **Staph infections**
  - **Eczema herpeticum** (if herpes virus gets in)
  - **Impetigo** (a contagious bacterial skin infection)

### 4. **Delays Healing**
- Skin that’s constantly scratched takes **longer to heal**, making flares more frequent and prolonged.

### 5. **Can Cause Scarring or Hyperpigmentation**
- Especially in people with darker skin tones, chronic scratching can leave **dark or light patches** or permanent scars.

---

### What to Do Instead of Scratching:
- **Apply moisturizer** or a cold compress to soothe itch.
- **Tap or pat** the itchy area instead of scratching.
- Use **anti-itch creams** (e.g., hydrocortisone, menthol-based creams).
- Consider **oral antihistamines** if nighttime itching is a problem.
- Keep nails short to reduce damage if you do scratch.
