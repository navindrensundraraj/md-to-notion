# Acrylic Adhesive Heat Risks

---
**User:**

What are the risks of acrylic adhesive being used as a mold release film adhesive layer and subjected to heat up to 175 C for prolonged period

---
**Assistant:**

Using **acrylic adhesive** as a **mold release film adhesive layer** and subjecting it to prolonged heat exposure of **up to 175°C** (typical of transfer molding in semiconductor packaging like QFN) carries several risks due to the **thermal limitations and chemical stability** of acrylic adhesives.

Here’s a breakdown of the risks:

---

### 🔥 1. **Thermal Degradation**
- **Acrylic adhesives typically have service temperatures up to ~150°C**, with some high-performance variants reaching 175°C briefly.
- At **175°C for prolonged periods**, the adhesive may:
  - **Soften or flow**, reducing mechanical stability.
  - **Break down chemically**, producing decomposition by-products (e.g. acetic acid, aldehydes).
  - **Lose adhesion strength**, leading to delamination or shifting of the mold release film.

---

### 🧪 2. **Outgassing and Contamination**
- Thermal degradation can release **volatile organic compounds (VOCs)**, which:
  - **Contaminate the molding compound** or **exposed die surfaces**.
  - Contribute to **mold voids, delamination, or popcorn cracking** during later reflow or reliability tests.
  - May interfere with **wire bonding or plating** due to surface contamination.

---

### 🧯 3. **Reduced Mold Release Performance**
- Acrylic adhesive residue may:
  - Remain on the **leadframe or DAP** after mold release.
  - Cause **poor solderability** or **increased contact resistance**.
  - Interfere with **bond pad cleanliness** or leadframe adhesion.

---

### ⚠️ 4. **Reliability Risks**
- Over time, degraded adhesive can compromise:
  - **Package moisture resistance** (higher MSL level).
  - **Thermal cycling endurance**.
  - **Mechanical integrity** (adhesive cracking or shrinkage).

---

### ✅ Recommendations:
- Use **high-temperature-resistant adhesives** designed for molding processes, e.g.:
  - **Silicone-based** adhesives (better thermal stability).
  - **Epoxy-based** if permanent bonding is acceptable.
  - Or specially formulated **heat-resistant acrylics** rated for >175°C continuous use.
- Validate via **TGA (Thermogravimetric Analysis)** and **outgassing tests** (e.g. GC-MS) to confirm long-term thermal stability.

---

If you're considering acrylic for convenience or cost, it's important to verify the **Tg (glass transition temperature)** and **maximum service temperature** of the specific formulation, as generic acrylics will fail under semiconductor mold conditions.
