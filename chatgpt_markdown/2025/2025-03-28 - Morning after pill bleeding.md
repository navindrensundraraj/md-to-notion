Can morning after pill cause early period with watery brownish blood?

Yes, the morning-after pill can cause an early period with watery brownish blood. This is a common side effect due to the high dose of hormones (levonorgestrel or ulipristal) in the pill, which can temporarily disrupt your menstrual cycle. The brownish color comes from older blood being shed, and the watery consistency may be due to hormonal fluctuations.

Other possible effects include:  
- **Early or delayed period** – Your next cycle may come earlier or later than usual.  
- **Lighter or heavier bleeding** – Some women experience spotting or a heavier flow.  
- **Other symptoms** – Nausea, breast tenderness, mild cramping, or fatigue.

If your period is more than a week late, or you experience severe pain or prolonged bleeding, it’s a good idea to take a pregnancy test or consult a doctor.
