### ✅ **How to Reheat Pizza in an Air Fryer:**

1. **Preheat air fryer** (optional but recommended):  
   Set it to **160–180°C (320–350°F)** for 2–3 minutes.

2. **Place the pizza slice(s)** in the basket:  
   - Lay them flat in a **single layer**.  
   - Avoid overlapping slices.  
   - Line the basket with **parchment paper or foil** if you're worried about cheese dripping.

3. **Heat for 3–5 minutes**:  
   - Start checking around **minute 3** to avoid burning.  
   - Thin crust may only need 3 mins; thick crust or deep-dish might need closer to 5–6 mins.

4. **Let it cool for 1 minute** before eating:  
   The cheese will be super hot!

---

### 🔥 Tips:
- **Don’t use max heat** (like 200°C); it may burn the crust before the middle is warm.
- If reheating multiple batches, later slices may need less time since the fryer is already hot.
- For extra crispiness, you can spray a **tiny bit of oil** on the crust before air frying.

Let me know if you're working with frozen or refrigerated pizza — the method is slightly different.

### 🍕 **Air Fryer Method for Cold (Refrigerated) Pizza:**

#### 🔧 What you need:
- Air fryer
- Cold pizza (straight from fridge)
- Optional: parchment paper or foil, a bit of oil spray (for crispier crust)

---

### 🕒 **Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. **Preheat the air fryer** (optional but improves texture):  
   - Set to **170°C (340°F)** for 2–3 minutes.

2. **Place pizza slices in the basket**:  
   - **Single layer only**, no stacking.  
   - Use **parchment paper** or **foil** if you're worried about mess.  
   - Lightly spray crust with oil if you like it extra crisp.

3. **Heat at 170°C (340°F) for 4–5 minutes**:  
   - Thin crust: check at 3–4 min.  
   - Thick crust or heavily topped: may need 5–6 min.

4. **Check doneness**:  
   - Cheese should be melted and bubbling.  
   - Crust should be crisp but not hard.

5. **Cool for 1–2 minutes** and enjoy!

---

### ❗ Extra Tips:
- Don’t use high heat (e.g. 200°C) or the cheese might burn before the base is warm.
- If the toppings are very thick or it’s a deep-dish slice, reduce heat slightly and add a minute or two.
