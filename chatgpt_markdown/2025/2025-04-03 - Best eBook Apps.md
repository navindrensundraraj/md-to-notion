Librera Reader can play audiobooks and read eBooks with **Text-to-Speech (TTS)**. However, it does not have a built-in dedicated audiobook player like Smart AudioBook Player. Here’s how you can play audiobooks in Librera:  

### **Method 1: Playing Audio Files (MP3, M4B, etc.)**  
1. **Open Librera Reader** and go to the **Library** tab.  
2. Navigate to the **folder where your audiobook files are stored**.  
3. Tap on the **MP3, M4B, or other audio file** to play it.  
4. Use the **playback controls** to pause, rewind, or fast-forward.  

### **Method 2: Using Text-to-Speech (TTS) for eBooks**  
If you have an **EPUB, PDF, or MOBI file** and want it read aloud:  
1. Open the eBook in Librera.  
2. Tap the **menu (three dots)** in the reading interface.  
3. Select **"Text-to-Speech" (TTS)** to start reading aloud.  
4. Adjust the speech speed, voice, and language in the TTS settings.  

### **Limitations & Alternative**  
- Librera can play **audiobooks** but lacks features like bookmarks, playback speed adjustment, and auto-resume.  
- If you need a **better open-source audiobook player**, try **Voice Audiobook Player** ([GitHub](https://github.com/PaulWoitaschek/Voice)) alongside Librera.
