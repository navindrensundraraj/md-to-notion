2. Inventory Turnover Ratio: This ratio measures how quickly a company is able to sell its inventory. A high inventory turnover indicates efficient inventory management and strong sales, while a low ratio may indicate overstocked inventory or weak sales. 

3. Debtor Turnover Ratio: This ratio measures how quickly a company is able to collect payment from its debtors. A high debtor turnover ratio indicates efficient credit and collection policies, while a low ratio may indicate a higher risk of bad debts. 

When analyzing these ratios, it's important to compare them to industry averages and to consider factors such as the company's size and product offerings that may impact the results.
