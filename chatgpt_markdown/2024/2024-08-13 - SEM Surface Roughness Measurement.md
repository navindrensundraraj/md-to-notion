# SEM Surface Roughness Measurement

---
**User:**

Can SEM measure surface roughness

---
**Assistant:**

Yes, Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) can be used to measure surface roughness, but it typically does so indirectly. SEM provides high-resolution images of a surface, allowing detailed visualization of surface topography. By analyzing the images, especially with techniques like 3D reconstruction or using specialized software, the surface roughness can be estimated. However, SEM is more commonly used to provide qualitative assessments of roughness rather than precise quantitative measurements.

For more accurate quantitative measurements of surface roughness, techniques like Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM) or profilometry are usually preferred.
