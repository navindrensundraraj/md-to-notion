Neutrogena T/Gel and T/Sal are both over-the-counter treatments for scalp conditions like dandruff, psoriasis, and seborrheic dermatitis. Here's a comparison to help you understand their differences:

### Neutrogena T/Gel
- **Active Ingredient:** Coal Tar (0.5%).
- **Uses:** Primarily for dandruff, psoriasis, and seborrheic dermatitis.
- **Mechanism:** Coal tar slows down the growth of skin cells, reducing flaking, scaling, and itching.
- **Frequency:** Often used a few times a week; specific instructions depend on the severity of the condition.

### Neutrogena T/Sal
- **Active Ingredient:** Salicylic Acid (3%).
- **Uses:** Also targets dandruff, psoriasis, and seborrheic dermatitis.
- **Mechanism:** Salicylic acid is a keratolytic agent that helps remove scales and flakes by exfoliating the scalp.
- **Frequency:** Generally used several times a week, similar to T/Gel, but follow specific instructions based on your condition.

### Key Differences
- **Active Ingredients:** T/Gel uses coal tar, while T/Sal uses salicylic acid.
- **Mechanism of Action:** T/Gel focuses on reducing skin cell growth, while T/Sal works by exfoliating and removing scales.
- **Skin Sensitivity:** Some people may find coal tar more irritating than salicylic acid or vice versa, depending on personal skin sensitivity.

### Choosing Between Them
- **Scalp Condition:** If you have thick scales or plaques, T/Sal might be more effective due to its exfoliating properties. For general dandruff or milder conditions, T/Gel could be sufficient.
- **Skin Sensitivity:** Consider trying a small amount of each product to see which one your scalp tolerates better.
- **Doctor's Advice:** Consult a dermatologist for personalized recommendations, especially if you have a severe or persistent condition.
