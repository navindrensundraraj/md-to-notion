# Sunk Cost Fallacy Explained

---

Sunk cost fallacy

The sunk cost fallacy is the tendency to continue investing time, money, or effort into something simply because you've already invested in it, even if it's no longer the best decision. This mindset is based on a reluctance to "waste" previous investments, but it can lead to more significant losses over time.

For example, if you bought an expensive concert ticket but, on the day of the concert, you feel sick or don’t want to go, the sunk cost fallacy would make you feel obligated to attend simply because you’ve already paid for the ticket. Rationally, since the money is already spent (a "sunk cost"), the best decision would be to skip the concert if it no longer adds value or happiness to your day.

Overcoming this fallacy means recognizing that past investments shouldn't dictate current choices if they're no longer beneficial.
