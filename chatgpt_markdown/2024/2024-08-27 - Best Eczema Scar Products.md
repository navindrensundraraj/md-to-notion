# Best Eczema Scar Products

---
**User:**

Best skin healing products for eczema scars

---
**Assistant:**

For healing eczema scars, the goal is to reduce hyperpigmentation, improve skin texture, and promote overall healing. Here are some effective products:

1. **Cica (Centella Asiatica) Creams**:
   - Cica creams are known for their soothing and healing properties. They help repair skin and reduce scarring.
   - Examples: **La Roche-Posay Cicaplast Baume B5**, **Etude House Soon Jung Cica Balm**.

2. **Silicone Gel or Sheets**:
   - Silicone products are clinically proven to reduce scars' appearance. They create a protective barrier that hydrates the skin and allows scars to heal better.
   - Examples: **Mederma Silicone Scar Sheets**, **ScarAway Silicone Scar Gel**.

3. **Vitamin E Oil**:
   - Vitamin E is an antioxidant that can help with scar healing by promoting collagen production and moisturizing the skin.
   - Example: **Bio-Oil**, **Palmer’s Skin Therapy Oil**.

4. **Aloe Vera Gel**:
   - Aloe vera has soothing and moisturizing properties that can help lighten scars and improve skin healing.
   - Example: **Aloe Vera Gel by Nature Republic**, **Banana Boat Aloe Vera Gel**.

5. **Niacinamide (Vitamin B3) Serums**:
   - Niacinamide helps to reduce hyperpigmentation, even out skin tone, and improve skin texture, making it useful for treating post-inflammatory marks from eczema.
   - Example: **The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1%**, **Paula's Choice 10% Niacinamide Booster**.

6. **Hydrocortisone Cream** (for active eczema):
   - While not for scars directly, using hydrocortisone cream during flare-ups can reduce inflammation and prevent further scarring.
   - Example: **Cortizone-10**, **Aveeno 1% Hydrocortisone Anti-Itch Cream**.

7. **Scar Creams with Onion Extract**:
   - Onion extract can help to improve the texture and appearance of scars over time.
   - Example: **Mederma Advanced Scar Gel**.

For best results, it's important to use these products consistently and as directed. Additionally, keeping the skin well-hydrated and protected from the sun can help reduce the visibility of scars. If scars are severe or not responding to topical treatments, consulting a dermatologist may be necessary for advanced treatment options like laser therapy or chemical peels.
