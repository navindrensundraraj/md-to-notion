Why my towel becomes rough

---
**Assistant:**

Towels can become rough for various reasons. Over time, frequent washing and exposure to certain laundry products, like fabric softeners, can leave residues that affect the towel's texture. Hard water, which contains high mineral content, might also contribute to roughness. To maintain softness, try using less detergent, avoiding fabric softeners, and occasionally using vinegar or baking soda during washes to remove build-up. Additionally, tumble-drying with dryer balls or tennis balls can help fluff up and soften towels.

I thought using softeners make the fabric soft

---
**Assistant:**

While fabric softeners can make fabrics feel softer initially, they may leave residues that build up over time, leading to a decrease in absorbency and making towels feel rough. Some experts recommend avoiding fabric softeners with towels, especially those made of cotton, as they can impact the towels' ability to absorb moisture effectively. Instead, consider alternatives like white vinegar or baking soda during washes to maintain softness without compromising absorbency.

What about softeners on clothes

---
**Assistant:**

Fabric softeners can be used on clothes to impart a soft feel and reduce static cling. They work by coating the fabric fibers with a thin layer of chemicals. However, similar to towels, repeated use of fabric softeners might lead to a build-up that affects absorbency in materials like towels or athletic wear.

If you like the softness but want to avoid potential build-up, consider using fabric softeners sparingly or explore alternatives like dryer balls or dryer sheets. Additionally, choosing a laundry detergent with built-in fabric softening properties can be a more convenient option.

**User:**

What is dryer balls/sheets

---
**Assistant:**

Dryer balls are typically made of rubber, wool, or a similar material and are used in the clothes dryer to help soften fabrics, reduce wrinkles, and speed up drying by improving air circulation. They work by bouncing around in the dryer, separating clothes and preventing them from clumping together.

Dryer sheets, on the other hand, are thin sheets coated with fabric softeners and anti-static agents. They are added to the dryer with the clothes to reduce static, soften fabrics, and provide a pleasant scent.

Both dryer balls and sheets offer alternatives to traditional liquid fabric softeners, and they can be reused multiple times before needing replacement. They are particularly useful for people looking to reduce chemical exposure or avoid potential build-up on fabrics.
