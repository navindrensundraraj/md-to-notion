# Depressed Wire Causes

---
**User:**

Possible causes for depressed wire after molding in semiconductor

---
**Assistant:**

A depressed wire after molding in semiconductor packaging can occur due to several reasons during the wire bonding and molding processes. Here are the possible causes:

### 1. **Insufficient Wire Tension**
   - If the wire tension during the wire bonding process is too low, the wire might sag and become prone to deformation during molding.

### 2. **High Molding Pressure**
   - Excessive molding pressure can compress the wire, causing it to deform or depress against the substrate or die.

### 3. **Wire Bonding Defects**
   - Poorly formed wire loops or weak ball and stitch bonds can lead to a lack of structural integrity, making the wire susceptible to deformation during molding.

### 4. **Incompatible Mold Compound**
   - Using a mold compound with unsuitable flow or curing characteristics may apply uneven pressure on the wires, leading to depression.

### 5. **Improper Die Pad Design**
   - If the die pad height or layout does not provide sufficient clearance for the wire loops, the mold pressure can easily depress the wire.

### 6. **Improper Mold Design**
   - Molding equipment with inadequate clearance or poor venting can lead to localized high pressure, affecting wire integrity.

### 7. **Excessive Heat During Molding**
   - High molding temperatures can soften the wire or bond materials, making the wire more prone to deformation under pressure.

### 8. **Wire Material and Diameter**
   - The choice of wire material (e.g., gold, copper, or aluminum) and its diameter can affect its resistance to compression. Thinner wires or softer materials are more prone to depression.

### 9. **Improper Molding Process Parameters**
   - Issues like uneven clamp force, imbalanced mold flow, or inconsistent mold cure cycles can exert unequal pressure on the wires.

### 10. **Contamination**
   - Contaminants on the mold or wire can interfere with the molding process and cause uneven pressure distribution, leading to depressed wires.

### Solutions:
- **Optimize Wire Bonding:** Adjust wire tension, loop height, and bonding parameters.
- **Refine Molding Process:** Calibrate molding pressure, temperature, and curing time.
- **Select Appropriate Materials:** Use mold compounds and wire materials compatible with your process requirements.
- **Improve Equipment Design:** Ensure mold cavity and clamp design provide uniform pressure distribution.
- **Perform Regular Maintenance:** Keep equipment and materials free from contaminants.
