It's **not recommended** to use lemon on your **groin area**, especially over **white spots** or **itchy skin**. Here's why:

### ⚠️ Why You Should Avoid Lemon:
1. **Lemon is acidic** – It contains citric acid, which can **irritate sensitive skin** and worsen itchiness, inflammation, or even cause burning sensations.
2. **Skin barrier damage** – On already irritated or possibly infected skin, lemon may further damage the skin barrier.
3. **Photosensitivity** – Lemon can make your skin more sensitive to sunlight, leading to **darkening or burns**, especially in delicate areas like the groin.

---

### ✅ Safer Alternatives for Groin Itching and White Spots:
The white spots and itchiness may be due to:
- **Fungal infection** (like tinea cruris/jock itch)
- **Dry skin or eczema**
- **Post-inflammatory hypopigmentation**

Here’s what you can do instead:

#### 1. **Use an antifungal cream** (if it's jock itch):
- Look for over-the-counter creams with **clotrimazole**, **miconazole**, or **ketoconazole**.
- Apply twice a day for at least 2 weeks, even if symptoms improve early.

#### 2. **Keep the area dry and clean**:
- Gently wash with mild soap and water.
- Pat dry thoroughly, especially in skin folds.

#### 3. **Apply soothing moisturizers**:
- Use a fragrance-free, gentle **moisturizer or aloe vera gel** if skin is dry or irritated.

#### 4. **Avoid scratching**:
- Scratching worsens irritation and increases the risk of secondary infections.

---

### 🩺 See a doctor if:
- The itch or white patches persist >2 weeks
- You notice scaling, spreading, or burning pain
- There’s pus, swelling, or broken skin
