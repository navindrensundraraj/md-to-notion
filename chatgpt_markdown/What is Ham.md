**"Ham"** can refer to different things depending on the context:

### 1. **Food (Most common use)**
Ham is meat from the hind leg of a **pig**, typically:
- **Cured** (using salt or brine)
- **Smoked** (for added flavor)
- Can be **cooked or raw** depending on preparation

Types include:
- **Honey-baked ham** (sweet glaze)
- **Prosciutto** (Italian dry-cured ham)
- **Jamon** (Spanish dry-cured ham)
- **Cold cuts/deli ham** (used in sandwiches)

> ⚠️ In some religions (like Islam and Judaism), ham is considered *haram* or not permissible due to being pork.

---

### 2. **Slang or Expression**
- "**To ham it up**" means to **overact** or behave in an exaggerated, theatrical way.

---

### 3. **Technology (Ham Radio)**
- **Ham radio** refers to **amateur radio**: a hobby where people use designated radio frequencies to communicate worldwide.

**Turkey ham** is a processed meat product made from **turkey meat**, not pork.

Here’s what you should know:

### ✅ What is it exactly?
- It's made from **cured turkey thigh meat**.
- It’s seasoned and shaped to **look and taste similar to pork ham**, but it's **100% turkey**.
- Usually sold **pre-cooked** and **ready to eat** (often sliced like deli meat).

### ✅ Why do people eat turkey ham?
- **Pork-free** alternative (suitable for people who avoid pork for religious or dietary reasons).
- **Lower fat** and **fewer calories** than traditional pork ham.
- Popular in **Muslim-majority countries** or in **halal** and **kosher** diets (check certification).

### ✅ Nutrition & Labeling
- It’s still processed, so may contain:
  - Sodium
  - Preservatives
  - Flavor enhancers
- Always check the label to make sure it’s really **turkey-only** and not mixed with other meats.
